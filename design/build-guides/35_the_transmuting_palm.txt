MECHANICAL CONCEPT NOTES:
 - go monk but build WIS as primary stat; leverage artificer features (spell-slots, experimental elixir, +1 wraps of unarmed prowess) and feats 
   (fey-touched::bless) to increase chance to hit as we level up without boosting DEX
 - stack bless spell with boldness elixir (from alchemist subclass) to give ourselves +2d4 to all attack rolls and saving throws 
 - build WIS to make save DC for monk abilities (inlc. stunning strike) higher 
 - eventually take psion levels to get more spell slots, psionic modes (defense mode), psionic disciplines, and metamorph subclass 
 - metamorph flesh maul organic weapon imposes disadvantage on CON/STR saves --> stunning strike is a CON save, elemental attunement forced movement 
   is a STR save. So we can leverage this on every turn, and with flesh maul we can stunning strike very reliably. 
 - elements monk also gives us extra reach (to avoid getting in melee) and damage type flexibility (since we don't get empowered strikes until later) 
 - eventually, elements also gives us AOE dmg (through elemental burst) and a non-concentration fly speed (stride of elements). 
 - base monk class features give us WIS-based AC, mobility, and defensive features 
 - start out as lvl-01 artificer and get war-caster feat to get CON save proficiency and adv on CON saves; in combination with bless effects, it should 
   be very hard to break our concentration on our bless spell
 - take mind-whisperer origin feat in order to have WIS based cantrip/spell to cast at early levels and to seed psionic theme for later when we 
   take psion levels.
 - use war-caster + mind-whisperer to nerf saving throws of enemies that leave your reach (which can be extended by both elemental attunement and 
   extend limbs, potentially up to 15ft) making it even easier to land stunning strikes on them on subsequent turns (disadv. and -1d4 if they get 
   mind-slivered then flesh-mauled) 
 - use spell slots primarily to cast bless/make elixers of boldness; otherwise take defensive/utility transmutation/illusion/divination spells from 
   artificer and psion (some very good options in both classes) 
 - build doesn't do the most damage, but you will an excellent single-target controller, be very resilient, and have a decent amount of out of combat 
   utility 
THEMATIC CONCEPT NOTES: 
 - avatar the last airbender + the witcher + jedi master (yoda) + witch-doctor/shaman/apothecary
 - flavor alchemist subclass around the distillation/harnessing of primordial forces/forces of inner planes. Healing/boldness potions are distilled 
   energy from the positive energy plane. Flight/swiftness potions are from the elemental plane of air. Resilience potions from the elemental plane of 
   earth, etc. Taking the potions `transforms' your appearance in some way (e.g., glowing skin, floaty hair, stony skin), lending to the transmutative 
   theme of the multiclass
 - flavor the bless spell as something akin to `entering the avatar state' and then the bless spell + the boldness potion as a heightened version of 
   this state. 
 - alchemist potions are produced through shamanistic rituals that have a druidic flavor, wherein we siphon `blood/essence' of the inner planes 
   (and give thanks in a pseudo-religious way). Maybe you can even flavor them as `mantras' or `meditations' that we study each day in order to gain 
   certain boons. 
 - flavor fey-touched as `touched by the inner planes' where your magic is granted by an exposure to the primordial forces that shape the multiverse 
 - flavor psion flesh-maul weapon as `earth element', turning the hand into a rocky fist that deals extra damage and debilitates 
 - play this character's high WIS almost in a druidic-way. You are a kind of martial-arts sage that understands and leverages primal magic forces 
   to grant them supernatural powers in combat. 
 - as character progresses, you open a stronger connection to the inner planes, and become a conduit to their magic and power; as you do primal magic 
   transforms you mentally/physically and presents you with a moral quandary related to the fate of the material plane
 - aesthetics: elemental transmutation, primal/nature magic, tribal/shamanistic, bohemian, ritualism and meditation, anti-materialism
 - themes: asceticism, discipline, mental/physical fortitude and grace, tranquility, meditation/enlightenment, connection to elemental/inner planes,

NOTE: Alright, lvl-04 and we go monk-03 to get deflect attacks and our subclass. Deflect attacks is really nice since its damage mitigation scales with our DEX and monk level, both of which will increase significantly throughout our career. This will help us stay alive, even given our pretty modest AC. Now I probably wouldn't use the redirect option of deflect attacks, unless there was a super specific reason to try and deal as much damage as possible. But it's nice to have options. Instead, I would save my focus points to use on our new subclass feature -- elemental attunement. Elemental attunement, which is the signature feature of our subclass, lets us spend 1fp at the start of our turn (no action required) to give ourselves increased reach (10ft), flexible damage typing, and forced movement with our unarmed strikes. We even have a little built in synergy with mind-sliver, which we can cast with our action to debuff an enemy's STR saving throw, and then make an unarmed strike with our BA and push/pull them. Super nice. The extra reach and forced movement also provide substantial defensive utility, as we can hit creatures with standard reach without getting into melee (and then retreat with our fast movement/step of the wind) and push creatures away from us on hit if we are in melee (meaning we don't have to use BA's or fps to disengage). And this builds on the defensive utility we already have from dissonant whispers. For this build, this defensive utility is important, as we will have a very important concentration spell (bless) that we will want to maintain starting next level. Next, as an elements monk we also get to learn the elementalism cantrip, which is very thematic and adds to our out of combat flavor/utility. Thematically, I think I would RP our character taking the elements subclass as them having discovered some hidden/ancient monastic ritual that allows them to tap into the primal energies of the inner planes. Just give it a bit of a scholarly flavor, like we discovered this through poring over ancient scrolls, or meditating on the philosophies of the old monk's of forgotten temple. Something like that.

Thematically, I would definitely flavor the bless spell as something akin to `entering the avatar state,' wherein we open a connection to some fount of raw elemental power in order to enhance our and our allies' combat effectiveness.
