MECHANICAL CONCEPT NOTES
 - overall motivation: make a WIS-first gish character that isn't purely reliant on shillelagh. Not fair that INT/CHA based caster classes have 
   so many options for building martial characters that put mental stats first. Here we do it with a druid-monk multiclass and we get to make 
   one of the worst-ever subclasses (sun-soul monk) shine! 
 - in general we're a WIS-based `blaster' monk/druid that leverages bound-spells and class features to deal radiant/fire spell damage
 - we start monk-01 for unarmored defense, DEX/STR save proficiency (since we'll be in melee) and so that we can benefit from the herbalism tools 
   proficiency via our background (druids get it by default) 
 - we then go druid-04 (stars) and take spellfire adept to enable WIS-based combat via several features: 
   + druid-01 gives cantrips to enable WIS-based attacks (including shillelagh which we'll use once we get extra attack) 
   + druid-02 gives us wild-shape, which we can use to turn into a giant venomous snake (DEX=18) and augment our monk BA:unarmed strike (only at druid-02) 
   + druid-03 (stars) gives us xWIS free guiding bolts per day, which we can use to deliver damage via searing spellfire while sparing our spell 
     slots for healing via cure wounds (to offset the lack of hit dice) 
   + druid-03 (stars) also gives us lvl-02 spells, including flame blade, which we can use to increase our damage in a melee focused playstyle 
   + druid-03 (stars) gives us starry-form (dragon) which we can use to help maintain concentration on flame blade and starry-form (archer) 
     which we can use to give ourselves a WIS-based BA spell attack 
 - spellfire spark gives us sacred-flame as a default WIS-based ranged cantrip, it also let's us do sacred-flame xPB/day as a BA; we leverage 
   this to deal damage with our BA and provide more opportunities for delivering searing spellfire from spellfire adept 
 - critically, we get prayer of healing (PoH) via our spells of the mark when we get lvl-02 spells
 - PoH providing the benefits of a short rest means it: 
   + restores our monk focus points (fp) --> so between a short rest, uncanny metabolism, and PoH we can fully recharge fp x3 per day 
   + gives us an extra use of wild shape --> so between a short rest and PoH we can use wild shape 4x per day (once each combat) 
 - next, we take monk levels all the way to monk-08
   + monk-02 gives us monk's focus/unarmored movement for general utility/mobility/defense (we won't use FoB for damage) and 
             uncanny metabolism for fp restoration and extra healing  
   + monk-03 gives us radiant sun-bolt which we won't use (unless we can't cast spells), as well as deflect attacks for extra defense 
   + monk-04 gives us elemental adept, which will boost damage from our flame blade (and eventually from our burning hands attacks),
             note between this and spellfire adept we bypass resistance to both fire and radiant damage  
   + monk-05 gives us extra attack, allowing us to leverage shillelagh for a damage boost when we attack 
   + monk-06 gives us searing arc strike which represents a significant boost to our BA damage (upcast burning hands) 
   + monk-07 gives us evasion 
   + monk-08 gives us the resilient feat, which we use to cap our WIS. Monk-08 also means we can upcast burning hands at lvl-03 (5d6) with our BA 
 - In general, as we take these monk levels: 
   + from monk-02 to monk-04 we keep our base playstyle and rely on flame-blade + archer + WIS-based cantrips with damage increases only coming 
     from elemental adept (slim) 
   + at monk-05 we stop using flame blade, switch over to extra attack with shillelagh, and use our extra fp to do stunning strikes 
   + at monk-06 we stop using stunning strike, and start using searing arc strike for a nice damage boost 
   + at monk-08, between spellfire spark and searing arc strike, we can use WIS-based attacks for our action/BA every round 
 - once we stop needing starry-form in combat, we can start using chalice form out of combat with PoH/cure wounds for extra healing 
 - after we get to monk-08, we go back to druid all the way to druid-12
   + this is because we don't get anything good from continuing with sun-soul, and base-class monk features push us towards DEX/unarmed strikes 
   + going further into druid let's us get better/higher level spells spells we like as we go further in druid at each level include: 
     * lvl-03: sylune's viper, aura of vitality (synergizes w/chalice) 
     * lvl-04: fire shield, fount of moonlight, grasping vine, stoneskin, wall of fire 
     * lvl-05: antilife shell, alustriel's mooncloak, mass cure wounds 
     * lvl-06: elminster's effulgent spheres, sunbeam, reincarnate
   + druid-06 gets us cosmic omen, which we will always have a good use for, since we are both making attack rolls (w/shillelagh/guiding bolt) 
     and forcing saving throws (via burning hands) in every combat 
   + druid-07 gets us primal strikes which is a nice flat boost to our melee damage
   + druid-08 gets us an ASI we use to boost our DEX to 18 (increasing our AC, save, and attack mod w/radiant sun-bolt) 
   + druid-10 gets us twinkling constellations which makes using starry form in combat relevant again 
 - we finish out druid-12 and take another ASI to cap our DEX at 20, which makes our WIS- and DEX-based attack modes fully equivalent 

THEMATIC CONCEPT NOTES: 
 - pop-culture analogs: starfire from teen titans, super saiyans from DBZ meets, lunar soul + spellfire sorcerer, eleven from stranger things, okarun from dandadan
 - This character is like the primal magic equivalent of a planter, solar, or empyrean. Our power is of the heavens -- literally, as in the sun, moon, and stars -- 
   versus being from the upper planes.
 - Think of this character as a sorcerer in a monk/druid body. Our magic comes from a reservoir of `spellfire' within us, that we use to smite our foes, heal our allies 
   and fuel our overall martial prowess
 - This power is inherently connected to the cosmic/astral entities of the material plane (sun, moon, stars) and we manifest different aspects of their power; we are a star child
   (a la starfire from teen titans)
 - I imagine that this character was always recognized for their special connection to the weave; perhaps they were trained in privileged isolation, separated from normal 
   society in order to cultivate their remarkable gift (a la eleven from stranger things).
 - Our druidic/scholarly side comes from the book-learning, religious training, and inherent connection we have to the weave and the cosmic forces that shape the material plane. 
 - Our physical prowess derives from the strengthening effects of our spellfire spirit on our physical form. 
 - I think you could even imagine the `spellfire soul' this character harbors within them as a separate entity. A borderline personality disorder even. Our baseline orientation 
   is to be gentle, caring, and seek to preserve and foster life. But when we are pressed into dangerous situations or overcome by stress/fear/emotion, we manifest the 
   harmful aspects of our power, transform, and use them to wreak havoc and destruction. As we progress in our journey, we gain more and more control over these different aspects 
   of our power, and learn to harness them according to our will and true inner motivations 
 - themes (TBD): the weave, radiance, fire, astral/cosmic entities (sun/moon/stars), primal spirituality, incarnate magic, power from the spirit/body, cosmic order/harmony, cherish life
 - traits (TBD): awkward, introverted, gentle, timid, naive, kind, honest, dutiful/devoted, protective, perceptive/observant, intuitive, caring, beautiful, slow but terrible anger

NOTE: Lvl-01 and we start out monk-01. We do this for a couple of reasons. First, we prefer the saving throw proficiencies we get with monk (STR/DEX) to those we get with druid (WIS/INT). This is because we're going to be (mostly) a melee fighter, and so we quite like STR save proficiency (especially with our low STR score). In addition, we won't be boosting DEX until much later levels, so getting DEX proficiency now helps a lot. Getting STR/DEX at character creation also means we can take the resilient feat to boost our WIS and gain WIS save proficiency later on. Going monk first also means we can get an extra tool proficiency. We can get herbalism kit proficiency from our background and cartographer's tools from monk. If we started druid, we would get herbalism kit proficiency from both the base class and our background, which is a bit inefficient. We can use the herbalism kit to craft healing potions and additional healer's kits (given time and money) and we can use the cartographer's tools to make maps that can help us navigate as we explore (actually quite handy). Between our healer's kits, our free cast of cure wounds from mark of healing, and the potion of healing we picked up at character creation via gold buy, we will be an effective healer even without spell slots. And between this healing and the damage reduction we get from spellfire spark we will be pretty resilient. Finally, going monk first also gets us a better set of starting skill proficiencies. Between class, background, and species we will have proficiency in: acrobatics, insight, medicine (+1d4 from mark of healing), perception, and stealth. Plus, with our high WIS + maps from cartographer's tools we can also do pretty well with survival. Very nice. In terms of combat, at this level we are just a basic monk wielding a quarterstaff. However, spellfire spark does give us the additional versatility of using sacred-flame for range. Damage-wise, we do: 
1d8 + 1d6 + 2DEX(3) = 14

For our cantrips, we take guidance for utility, produce flame for a source of light (we are human and lack darkvision), and resistance for additional damage reduction (can stack w/magic absorption from spellfire spark). We'll switch out guidance for shillelagh when we get our subclass in a few levels (which grants us guidance as an auto-preparation). As a lvl-01 druid, we also get the druidic feature which gives us the ability to speak, read, and write in druidic and gives us the speak with animals spell. Neither is super useful, but bonus utility is always appreciated. Next, we get spellcasting, hooray! We get 2 lvl-01 spell slots and can prepare 4 spells. Now, once we hit lvl-05, we'll want to preserve most of our lvl-01 spell slots to use for healing via cure wounds (since we'll be expending our hit dice to deal damage via searing spellfire). So we don't want to get attached to anything that we will have to stop using at later levels. We also want to stay within (as best possible) our `radiant starfire being' kind of theme. With this in mind, for preparations I went with detect magic, false life, jump, and protection from good and evil. Detect magic is great general utility. False life is from our mark of healing list and provides us with additional durability if/when we need it. False life also feels like it dovetails with the theme/idea that spellfire fortifies our body, and I like the idea of using false life in conjunction with magic resistance and the resistance cantrip to tank magic damage. It also works with the idea that, at baseline, we are a gentle soul and our magic is primarily focused on restoring and preserving life. Jump is a great mobility spell, and will be our only mobility option for the next several levels. It also fits just fine with the `magically-enhanced body' kind of idea and feels very monk-like to boot. Finally, protection from evil and good is more about the thematic consistency than the mechanical utility. It's not a spell that I would expect to cast very often (or at all, ideally), but it does fit with our identity as a protector, guardian, and preserver of life. Furthermore, I think if we cast it on ourself we can view it as spellfire bolstering our spirit and mental fortitude. But like I said, we're going to want to use most lvl-01 slots on cure wounds soon, at which point all of these spells will become more like background options. So don't get too attached. And of course, as a druid, we can change our spells out at each LR, so if there's something else that seems more useful, prepare that. In terms of damage, nothing has changed since lvl-01. But our spellcasting has made us significantly more durable and increased our capacity as a healer.

NOTE: Ok lvl-03 and we go druid-02. We get wildshape, wild companion, an additional lvl-01 spell slot, and one new spell preparation. First, on wildshape. We know 4 forms. I like fox for the burrow speed, keen hearing, and darkvision (we already have good stealth). I like the cat for the climb speed and the ability to be innocuous in human/urban settings. I like the elk for it's large size (can serve as a mount) and it's high STR score. And then the giant venomous snake is our default combat option. The reason we like the giant venomous snake is because it is the only CR 1/4 creature that has a +4 to DEX, which is currently greater than the +3 we have in human form. Since we retain class features when we wild-shape, but use the physical statistics of our beast form, this means that if we can still use our monk's martial art's to make unarmed strikes in wild-shape, gaining a +1 to hit and damage via the venomous snake's high DEX. Now, we will literally *only* do this here at lvl-03, so if I wanted to prioritize the character's thematic consistency, I would probably just use my wild-shapes to summon wild companions and scout/give myself advantage on some attacks. But if we're min-maxing for damage (and we are), the venomous snake is definitely the way to go. Not only does being a venomous snake boost our speed up to 40ft, but it also gives us a bite attack that deals 1d4+4 piercing plus 1d8 poison (11) damage on hit and we can still make an unarmed strike (e.g., with our snake tail) as a BA for an additional 1d6+4 (7.5) damage. This means while we are in snake form our DPR potential for a single round goes up to 18.5 (from 14) and we have a greater chance to hit. This equates to a nice bump in DPR while wild-shaped. Because we also still benefit from our monk's unarmored defense feature while wild-shaped, the boost to our DEX while in snake form also gives us a +1 to our AC (up to 17). Super nice. With 2 uses of wild-shape per day, plus one recharged from a short rest, we can do this in 3 combats per day. Ideally we can precast wild shape before combat so we don't have to burn our BA transforming on turn-01, but at druid-02 we can only remain wild-shaped for 1hr, so this might be tricky. We also can't cast spells while wild-shaped, so if we want to do any healing then we should stay in human form. When not wild-shaped, we should just go back to our standard monk tactics -- i.e., attack::quarterstaff + BA::unarmed-strike in melee and magic::sacred.flame + BA::sacred.flame_{spellfire.spark} in range -- or using actions/BA's to heal as needed. Like I said, I think if I we're playing this character, I might just skip using wild-shape in combat at this level and make the most out of wild companion. However, for the sake of our DPR calculation, I'm going to assume that we are using wild-shape to turn into a venomous snake in 3 out of 4 daily combats, and make standard melee monk attacks in the final combat. Ok moving on. For our new spell preparation, I went with healing word. If we do happen to be in combat and aren't wild-shaped, then we might like to have a ranged BA heal to help keep our allies up in the fight. Healing word does that. Longstrider is the other spell I'm particularly drawn to here, but with only 3 spell slots I think leaning into our identity as a healer makes more sense. Finally, we also get to learn lesser restoration and cast it once for free per LR via our mark of healing origin feat. With our current spell slots, we can only cast it once (and we won't always need to do that) but it's some nice bonus utility and helps to cement our healer identity. Alright, somewhat strange level here, but one that does push our power-curve upwards to a very nice place for tier-01, damage wise. Very nice.

NOTE: Haha, wow. Looks like such a simple level, but there's more than meets the eye here, I promise. Lvl-20 and we go druid-12 to pick up an ASI/feat. My honest recommendation here is to pick up an ASI and bump our DEX to 20. And I say this despite knowing that we could take an epic boon feat instead. And, depending on how you look at it, some of the epic boons would probably be more mechanically powerful. Boon of dimensional travel would let us zip around teleporting every round. Boon of fate would give us additional protection on saving throws to maintain concentration. Boon of recovery would give us a pool of dice to use for healing in lieu of our own hit dice, allowing us to focus our healing on our allies. All totally good options, and I actually really like the idea of boon of recovery and think it would be very appropriate for this character. But here's the thing -- our entire career we have been a monk that has suffered from the fact that we have chosen to build WIS as our primary stat. Now, I think we've done a good job managing this and leveraging spells and abilities that allow us to deal damage using WIS, even while pursuing a martial playstyle. But it hasn't been easy. Boosting DEX to 20 means that we can now take advantage of some of the cool parts of our monk kit that we haven't been able to use much thus far. Right off the bat, for example, we no longer need shillelagh. Now, we lose a bit of damage from the damage die downgrading from 2d6 (7) to 1d8 (4.5). But freeing up a BA in nearly every combat with which to deal damage is much more impactful. And of course, if we have time, we can just precast shillelagh to retain he higher weapon damage. That said, we're also no longer locked to a club our quarterstaff at this point. We could use a scimitar, a dagger, or any other simple/light-martial weapon that is available to us. With the (likely) availability of strong magic weapons at this level, this could also represent a meaningful damage boost for us. Note, however, that we can only apply elemental weapon to a non-magical weapon. So we'd probably want to retain a quarterstaff for that. In general, I make no assumptions about magic weapons when I do my builds, but do try to acknowledge that having the flexibility to use them is a bonus. This character hasn't had much flexibility to use magic weapons up until this point, but boosting DEX to 20 instead of taking a different half feat/epic boon does provide this. Boosting DEX to 20 also increases our armor class to 20. While this isn't massive, it is important as a martial character that wants to concentrate on spells to have as high an AC as possible. And while I would hope that we've been able to acquire some magic items that increase our AC by now (e.g., bracers of defense, cloak/ring of protection, etc.), AC boosting items are harder to come by for a character that doesn't want to wear armor or carry a shield. So the boost to unarmored AC is actually quite meaningful. Finally, with DEX=20, we can now make efficient use of flurry-of-blows to make 4 attacks per turn every turn during combat and significantly boost the damage we deal with fount of moonlight. This allows us to shift BA resources across combats in a way that not only significantly increases our overall damage output, but also allows us to use stunning strike in almost every round of combat. Now, you might think that we surely will want to switch out FoM for CME now that we can make so many attacks but we actually don't. This is because we still rely on FoM as a delivery mechanism for fueled spellfire. In conclusion, boosting DEX to 20 actually does *a lot* for us. It (i) boosts our AC, (ii) frees up our BAs to deal more damage, (iii) enables magic weapons in some combats, (iv) strengthens FoM damage, and (v) allows for significantly greater use of stunning-strike. All in all, I think these improvements are worth more than we would get out of any single epic boon. If your DM is nice, they'll also let you apply your primal strike damage (1d8) to unarmed strikes you make with flurry-of-blows while in starry form. This will help boost damage in combat 1 (though probably not RAW). Then, our new combat tactics at this level look as follows:
