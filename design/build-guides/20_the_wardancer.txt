CONCEPT NOTES: The main idea behind this build is to be an amazing martial battlefield controller, and a gish that fights primarily with unarmed strikes. Taking a 1-level monk dip will get us a reliable BA unarmed strike, and the ability to make grapple and shove attempts using DEX. Dance bard will give us the ability to make unarmed strikes as reactions, and to reposition our allies. And also the ability to cast spells. Going battlemaster will give us access to weapon masteries (push), as well as battlemaster maneuvers (disarm, push). By the time we're level 9, we'll be able to do a pretty nasty combo where we disarm a creature, grapple it, and then drag it away from it's weapon, rendering it pretty useless. The tavern brawler, grappler, and charger feats will all add to this type of playstyle, while the fey-touched feat will give us some extra mobility and
increase our ability to grapple/control key targets. 

BACKGROUND NOTES: 
 - more than meets the eye: delicate little dancer-girl on the surface; tough as nails fighter underneath 
 - a bit of a lush: our character fancies a drink. our fighting style is almost drunken-master-esque,
                    bobbing and weaving with dancer's grace
 - a bit of a tease: charming, manipulating and deceiving those who underestimate us
 - unrestrained: fast, free-flowing movement to accompany an itinerant lifestyle. Move uninhibited, like the wind.

ADDENDA (NOTES TO SELF): 
We have `cloud of daggers' as our key 2nd-level spell. While more situational, I think hold person would potentially be better, as paralyzed creatures automatically fail STR and DEX saves. Two of our key Battle Master maneuvers (pushing attack, disarming attack) are STR saves, so paralyzing opponents first would guarantee some key combos. Of course, you also get critical hits on melee attacks against paralyzed enemies, which would help our damage. Only downside is that the save would rely on our spell casting mod (CHA) which will be low until very late level. So in that sense, CoD, which doesn't require a save, is better. 

It looks like grappling has switched from a contested athletics check to a straight saving throw in 2024 PHB. But it also seems like athletics might be what you/enemies use to try and escape a grapple. Below we go with proficiency and expertise in athletics to promote grappling, but if we don't have to do that then choose another skill. We also take fey-touched in order to get hex. But we might not really need hex as much (or want that feat at all) if imposing disadvantage on STR-checks doesn't help us with our grapple. 

Grappler + spike growth is a broken combination. It also synergizes with our push control abilities. We can get spike growth starting at bard-10 with magical secrets. So potentially consider getting this and switching out cloud of daggers. They're both thematically consistent. But with it's larger AOE and better damage scaling, I think it is a superior option to cloud of daggers in most situations.

NOTE: Ok level-1 and we're going to start out monk-1. But first a few notes on character creation. We're going with wood elf for our species to get 35ft of movespeed, and access to the longstrider spell at level-3. Druidcraft and Pass without trace at level-5 are also nice, but we're mostly interested in the first two. As an elf, we also get proficiency in perception, which is great. From our background, we take proficiency with calligrapher's tools so that we can (eventually) craft spell scrolls. We take cartographer's tools so that we can (eventually) craft maps. These proficiencies are both thematically consistent with our background as a traveling performer (e.g, making signs to advertise our show w/calligrapher's tools, making maps as we travel from town to town). In terms of skill proficiencies, our background gives us proficiency in acrobatics (we're a dancer, afterall) and performance (traveling performer). As a monk we choose to be proficient in stealth -- because we have high DEX and stealth is very useful -- and athletics -- since we're eventually going to be doing a lot of grappling (see addendum above). Finally, for equipment, start out with a shortbow (even though we ideally won't be using it) and your artisan's tools. Also get a pan flute for when we eventually take bard levels. 

Ok so why are we starting out monk-1 here? First, the saving throw proficiencies (STR, DEX) are better than what we'd get with Bard, and we really want martial arts. In particular, we really like that martial arts lets us make BA unarmed strikes right off the bat, and that it let's us use DEX instead of STR for grapple and shove checks. We have proficiency in athletics and high DEX, so we're already capable of grappling quite effectively. Martial arts also dovetails really nicely with our background/level-0 feat, which is tavern brawler. 

Tavern brawler does a few really nice things for us. First, it lets us shove a creature (no more than one size larger than us) 5ft when we hit it with an unarmed strike as part of the attack action x1 per turn. This means that we can effectively kite creatures with standard reach by doing: BA:unarmed-strike + attack:unarmed-strike(shove). By pushing enemies away with our last unarmed strike, we move ourselves out of their reach, allowing us to move away from them without provoking AoOs. This 'disengage' combo is particularly important for our character right now at level-1 because our AC is pretty low (14). Next, tavern brawler also let's us reroll 1's when we roll damage for unarmed strikes (have to use new roll). This is an awesome feature for us since the majority of our damage will come from unarmed strikes. Finally, tavern brawler lets us use furniture as club weapons. This is nice because both clubs and greatclubs are monk weapons, so we can use them with DEX and still do BA unarmed strikes. For now, we should stay unarmed so that we can shove with our unarmed strikes. But eventually, the greatclub will be a weapon that we use, so this component of tavern brawler means that we will rarely be far from one of our favorite weapons. 

In terms of combat equipment -- as a monk (and, eventually dance bard) we don't wear armor so no need to deal with that. In terms of weapons, I would get a shortbow with my starting gold. The shortbow isn't a monk weapon, but gives us a good ranged option (range 80/320) that we are proficient with. 

In terms of combat tactics -- it's pretty simple here at level-1. Use you high speed to get into melee. Use the kiting combo to deal damage while staying out of reach of heavy hitters. And use grapples and shoves to try and control enemies where appropriate. Alright, whew! Level-1 out of the way. On to level-2.

In general, getting bardic inspiration and spellcasting gives us some new support and combat utility options. We only have two d6's for inspiration right now, but that will change in a few levels. Bardic inspiration also got some reworks in the 2024 PHB: creatures granted inspiration now have an hour (rather than 10m) to use it, you can roll the inspiration die after you fail a d20 test (rather than before) and you can grant inspiration to creatures who can hear or see you (not just see). 

In terms of spells, for cantrips I took friends and blade ward. Friends has been reworked in 2024. It no longer works automatically, and instead requires the target make a WIS saving throw or be charmed. But it also no longer alerts the target that you (tried to) charm them, or makes them hostile to you afterwards. Overall, I think this is a big positive improvement, as it means we can use friends in social settings without as much fear of blowing everything up. Second, we take blade ward. Blade ward was also reworked for 2024 and is now cast as a reaction to a creature targeting you with a melee attack roll. When you cast it, you force the creature to make the attack with disadvantage. This is a great option for us, with our low AC, and will probably be the best default use of our reaction in combat for now. In terms of level-1 spells, we get to prepare 4 as a level-1 bard. I think cure wounds is a good emergency survivability option, and will help us make it through with our crappy AC. Silvery barbs is another good support use for our reaction. In general, this character will be doing a lot of work on their reaction, so this spell is consistent with the overall playstyle we'll be going for. Faerie-fire is a decent concentration option for now, potentially giving us advantage on all attacks against a creature who fails the save. It's also consistent with our overall fey/elf vibe. And finally, heroism. Heroism gives us CHA THP per turn, so only 2 right now, which isn't great. But it's again, since we're going to be in melee as a bard with AC-14, we need survivability options to get us through. Heroism is good in that role for now.

NOTE: Ok we're level-4 and we go bard-3 to get our subclass. As we've previewed a bit by now, we're going to go with the college of dance. Thematically, I would think of this more like our character is learning to develop a dance-based fighting style (like capoeira) versus doing any sort of traditional dance on the battlefield. We get a number of nice features from dance bard. First we get the `dazzling footwork' feature, which has three components. First, it gives us an improved unarmored AC based on our DEX and CHA, which boosts our AC up to 15 (still not great, but better). Second, it lets us make an unarmed strike whenever we expend a bardic inspiration (BI) die as part of an action, bonus action, or reaction. This means that we no longer have to choose between using our BA to make an unarmed strike or deal out a BI, we can do both. It also means we can make unarmed strikes when we use our other new feature, inspiring movement, with our reaction (more on that in a sec). The third part of dazzling footwork is that it lets us use DEX for attack rolls with our unarmed strikes (as a monk we already do that) and it lets us use the BI die for damage rolls. Right now our BI die and martial arts die are the same (both d6's) but this does mean that we can scale the damage of our unarmed strikes without having to take more levels in monk (whew!). Ok returning to the second new feature we get -- inspiring movement. This is a really cool feature, in my opinion. Inspiring movement says that whenever an enemy ends their turn within 5ft of an ally who is within 60ft of us, we can expend a BI as a reaction and (i) move up to 1/2 our speed, and (ii) the ally can move a number of feet equal to 5 times the roll we get on our BI die. Of course, expending a BI in this way also allows us to proc our the `agile strikes' component of the dazzling footwork feature, so we can also make an unarmed strike. In practice, I think it will be very common for the prerequisites for inspiring movement to be satisfied (usually at least some enemies are within 5ft of one of your allies). So this essentially amounts to a quite reliable way to reposition ourselves, our allies, and make unarmed strikes with our reaction. Very powerful. Right now we can only do this 2x per long rest, but that's going to change soon. Also, note that inspiring movement has some good synergy with grappling. If we are able to grapple a creature on our turn, we can drag it to a favorable location inspiring movement with our reaction on someone else's turn. In combination with our improved AC (15), great athletics (+7) and high speed (45ft if we cast longstrider, 55ft with kinetic jaunt), I think here at level-4 grappling officially becomes a great option for controlling and reposition single targets. Use it to maximum effect. 

At bard-3 we also get another prepared spell (6) and we can prepare two level-2 bard spells. My picks are kinetic jaunt and mirror image. Kinetic jaunt is a really interesting spell that provides uniquely good utility for our character, and is certainly very thematic for a dance bard -- it even says `you move with dancelike steps' in the spell description. So how does it work? Well it's a bonus action to cast and requires concentration (for up to 1m). When we cast it, our speed increases by 10ft (so base 45ft, 55ft with longstrider), our movement doesn't provoke opportunity attacks, and we can move through other creatures space's without imposing any movement penalties. This is going to be great for our lock-down grapple move, as it makes it easier for us to into melee with key targets and/or drag them back to wherever we need them to be once they're grappled. This spell will also become even more useful next level, so just hold on. For our second level-2 spell, I think we should take mirror image. Even though our AC increased, it still isn't great, and mirror image is an awesome non-concentration survivability spell. After it's rework for the 2024 PHB, enemies only get a chance to hit your mirror images *when they hit you with an attack*. So each mirror image is essentially protected by your base AC. With mirror image up and blade ward handy as a reaction, I think we can feel good about getting up close to and grappling even some of the nastier enemy combatants we will encounter. Finally, for level-1 spells, I dropped faerie fire and kept our healing, reaction, and out-of-combat spells. Faerie fire is thematic, but it just won't be worth our concentration at higher levels. By contrast, healing word is pretty much always useful for resurrecting allies, and upcasting cure wounds is (now in 2024) a good burst heal. Silvery barbs is also just very good, and gives us even more options to control with our reaction.

NOTE: Ok so we're level and we go bard-4. We get our first ASI/feat, and we're going to go with fey touched. As a half-feat, fey touched let's us bump our CHA to 16. This is big for us, as it means we get a +1 to our unarmored AC (which we sorely needed), and an additional use of bardic inspiration. We also get to learn misty step and a 1st-level spell from the enchantment or divination schools (assuming fey touched works the same as it did in Tasha's). Misty step is great for us. It's fey themed, and gives us additional mobility which, as a melee fighter, we really like. For our other fey touched spell, as a melee fighter who will (eventually) make upwards of 4 attacks per round and is interested in grappling, hex is the best option. Hex gives us an extra 1d6 necrotic damage each time we hit a hexed enemy, and it let's us impose disadvantage on STR ability checks, which include grapple checks. It is a bonus action to cast, which makes our BA a bit crowded (currently we can do the following on our BA: unarmed strike, bardic inspiration, hex, healing word, misty step, kinetic jaunt). We'll eventually have better things to concentrate on, but for single target damage against a grapple-able enemy, it will always be a very good option, and it's nice to have good options that don't use high-level spell slots. Plus we get to cast it once per day for free, so that's nice. 

At bard-4 we also get another cantrip -- I took message for infiltration/deception synergy, but pick your favorites -- and we get to prepare another bard spell. Here I took cloud of daggers (CoD). Cloud of daggers is a concentration spell that conjures a 5ft cube of swirling spectral daggers that deal damage (4d4 slashing at lvl-2) to enemies when they enter the cube for the first time on a turn, or if they end their turn there. In general, I think the thematics of this spell are really cool, and perhaps fitting for our character. For example, I might flavor the daggers as swirling, spectral, dark-purple fey-energy spikes -- and have our character `dance' enemies into the cloud in order to damage them. Very cool. There are also a couple of key mechanical features of CoD for our character. First, there is no save against taking damage from the cloud. So even though our CHA won't be amazing moving forward (+3 until very late), we can still cast this spell and it will always do full damage if we can get enemies in it. Second, it upcasts reasonably well, adding another 2d4 damage for each level above 2nd. The last reason we really like cloud of daggers is because of our character's propensity for grappling, but this requires a little bit of explanation. In the new 2024 PHB, the rules around `dangerous area' emanation spells (like CoD) were changed. It used to be that creatures took damage when they entered the area for the first time on a turn, or when they *started* their turn there. This led to a lot of comps with CoD where you would cast it right behind an enemy, then find a way to shove them into it on your turn, so that they would take the damage both on your turn and when they started their turn there. This is no longer the case. Now it seems that enemies take damage whenever they enter the dangerous area on a turn and if they *end* their turn in the area. So this means that if we push someone into our cloud of daggers, they can just move out of it on their turn and avoid taking the damage a second time. Ok, but this spell is still going to be really good for us. Why? Well right now we can still take one turn to go attack and grapple an enemy. Then, once we have them grappled we can cast CoD on them on our next turn. This will proc the damage once when we first summon the cloud, and then they will take the damage again if they can't escape our grapple on their turn. So we can't do it all in one turn, but because we like to grapple, we can still get enemies to take the damage twice each round with a little bit of setup. The utility of CoD is also going to improve when we eventually take fighter levels and we get weapon masteries and action surge. So just hold tight. 

Finally as a level-5 wood elf we also get to learn pass without trace (PWT) and cast it for free once per day. Combined with our proficiency in stealth, this makes us an excellent infiltrator and one who can also help the party. It's a great bound spell to get and deepens our fey/elf-magic theme. Ok that's it for level-5, moving on.

This is, of course, quite useful. We we're probably doing ok before with catnap and font of inspiration in terms uses of bardic inspiration per day. But increasing our CHA to 18 here means -- assuming we get a 1-2 short rests and cast catnap per day -- that we will have anywhere from 12-16 uses of our bardic inspiration per day. So assuming a standard 3 encounters per adventuring day, we would be able to use all 4 of our inspiration die in each encounter. Great. Ok, that's it for level-19. On to the final stop!

NOTE: Finishing off with level-20, and I think we go bard-13. Nothing changes for us, except that we get to prepare 1 additional spell, and we get access to a 7th level spell slot. Obviously, there are a number of very powerful options that we could go with, but I'm going to say that we should take simulacrum. Now, simulacrum has been nerfed in the 2024 PHB -- most notably they made it so that your simulacrum can no longer take short or long rests. But..., I don't think this means that the simulacrum can't be targeted by the catnap spell. Ok so basically, what simulacrum does is give us a duplicate of ourselves -- or some other beast or creature, but I'm assuming we cast it on ourselves whenever we can -- that has half our hit points, but all of our same spells and abilities (minus the 7th level slot we used to cast it). The reason I like this spell is because it is the sort of spiritual evolution of mislead, which we already had in our kit. It also has very awesome uses in and out of combat. For combat use specifically, it essentially doubles our uses of all our key bardic inspiration and battlemaster maneuvers. And it means that if we bring our simulacrum with us in combat, we can have two very powerful battlefield controllers and melee strikers wreaking havoc. I mean, think of some of the combinations -- two creatures with YRP/ashardalon's stride and inspiring movement, two melee attackers with greater invisibility, mirror image, and blink up, two creatures capable of grappling and dragging. One of you can concentrate on spike growth while another can concentrate on YRP and zoom around and knock creatures into the spiked area. You can both cast mislead to create two illusory doubles for truly nutty out-of-combat deception shenanigans. It's level 20, so there's bound to be a lot of really crazy stuff happening. And there's just so many good uses of this spell, many of which are particularly strong given the nature of our character.
