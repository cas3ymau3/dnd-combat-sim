MECHANICAL CONCEPT NOTES
 - go dwarf + tough at character creation in order to be able to dump CON so we can invest in DEX, WIS, AND CHA. 
 - in general: you are not a true caster; use spells to buff martial abilities, concentrate on spells only when useful, but don't 
   care if our concentration is broken
 - start fighter-01 to get CON save proficiency; eventually when we are gaining advantage on saving throws via brutality each turn, 
   we can concentrate on cleric spells to boost damage; we also get dueling fighting style for a nice damage boost during early levels
 - immediately take warlock-01 to get pact of the blade and CHA-based true-strike 
 - then go war cleric and take shadow-touched to get war priest, magic weapon, prayer of healing, and wrathful smite 
 - we use war priest (w/prayer of healing) + wrathful smite to weaponize our BA; we use magic weapon and dueling 
   fighting style to boost flat damage from weapon attacks; we also get a bonus to true-strike damage from cantrip scaling which will 
   help prop up our damage in tier-02 while we're making our way to extra attack; action surge also helps with this. 
 - with WIS=16, we can use war priest 9x per LR (assuming 1 SR and 1 PoH); with 4 lvl-01 cleric spell slots, plus a pact magic slot 
   that recovers on a short rest (and with PoH) *and* a free-cast from shadow-touched, we can use wrathful smite up to 8x per LR. 
   Between this and war priest, we should have a way to weaponize our BA in all 16 rounds of combat/day. Excellent.  
 - defensively, we go sword and board with medium armor and a 15 DEX to have a respectable 18 AC; if we really need it, we can also 
   use shield of faith, though we won't be able to concentrate reliably until later levels
 - At level-08 we get brutality, now we can use a sap weapon (longsword) and also give it the vex property when we attack (with the 
   bluff option). This also gives us advantage on the next saving throw we make until our next turn, which is a huge defensive buff
   and one which we can also use to maintain concentration on spells.
 - With prayer of healing and CHA=20, we can use brutality 15x per LR (assuming 1 short rest and 1 PoH); this is almost every turn
   So now we should be attacking with a +1 weapon with advantage consistently, and making most saving throws with advantage
 - Keep going with fighter until fighter-06 to get extra attack and mage slayer (awesome feat + very thematic + synergizes w/prayer of 
   healing). Once we get extra attack, stop using true-strike. Now that we've capped CHA, all our future feats we put into DEX in order 
   to boost our DEX save, AC, and initiative. 
 - After fighter-06, we go back to cleric to get cleric-06, which gets us lvl-03 spell slots (+2 magic weapon) and war god's blessing. With 
   5 uses of channel divinity per day (3 base, +1 from SR, +1 from PoH) we can now use war god's blessing at the start of every combat to give 
   ourselves a +2 to AC (via non-concentration shield of faith). Between this, war priest, and wrathful smite, we should have a good use of our 
   BA in every round of combat. 
 - starting at level-14, we go back to fighter to pick up flourish parry (+CHA to AC against melee attacks with reaction), resilient DEX, indomitable, 
   and tactical master; tactical master let's us switch over to a rapier (vex weapon) and add the sap mastery to one attack per turn. This gives us 
   more uptime on vex (since it can proc on each attack). We can also concentrate on bless in order to boost our chance to hit/damage even further. 
 - at lvl-17 we get bolder brutalities. We can use the stagger option of bolder brutalities to give enemies disadv on our wrathful smites, potentially 
   making that a very effective tool against targets that can be frightened (another way of imposing disadv. on all attack rolls). 
 - finishing out, we go fighter-12 to get extra attack x2 and another feat. I pick epic boon of speed since we don't have any built in mobility bonuses
   and we like imposing disadvantage on opportunity attacks. But combat prowess is probably the best for offense. Medium armor master would give us 
   another base +1 to our AC, but that feels kind boring and we have a lot of ways to buff armor class when needed. Epic boon of fate could give us
   even more protection on saving throws (including CON). Pick your favorites. 
 - at lvl-20 we take cleric-07 to get a 4th level spell slot and blessed strikes, which adds another flat 1d8 (4.5) to our DPR. 
 - Overall, we get to play a `battlemage' style gish character that is mostly just a martial character in combat. We get to take WIS and CHA as primary 
   and secondary stats at character creation, which gives us a nice balanced set of skill and saving throw proficiencies 
 - We ultimately end up being able to buff our own attacks and impose disadvantage on enemies super reliably, while simultaneously building great 
   defensive utility and protection on saving throws. We won't ever be top tier in damage, but we'll be super effective in combat, while pulling off 
   a somewhat eccentric (albeit very thematically synergistic) multiclass.

Ok, so in terms of optimized combat, we have several new guiding principles. The first is that we want to use brutality::bluff to give ourselves advantage on our true-strike strike attacks. This is because true-strike is currently our most damaging attack, and also benefits the most from the increased crit chance that advantage provides. There are four ways we can set this up: 
  - (1) on our turn, use our BA to make a war priest attack, on hit apply brutality::bluff, then use our action to true-strike w/advantage
  - (2) on our turn, use action surge, take the attack action, apply brutality::bluff on hit, then use our second (normal) action to true-strike w/advantage 
  - (3) on an enemy's turn, use our reaction to make an opportunity attack, apply brutality::bluff on hit, then use our action on our turn to true-strike w/advantage
  - (4) on our turn do: true-strike + BA::wrathful-smite, and apply brutality::bluff on hit, then use our action our next turn to true-strike w/advantage 
Options (1) and (2) are the most reliable because they generate and use the advantage within the same turn. Options (3) and (4) depend on events between turns lining up correctly, but are still useful. Given this, we should use brutality charges according to the following paradigm:  
  - if we use our BA to make a war priest attack (first attack on our turn), then apply brutality::bluff to the attack on hit
  - if we use action surge to make an initial attack, apply brutality::bluff to the attack on hit
  - use brutality::bluff when we hit with attacks of opportunity on enemy turns 
  - use brutality::bluff on our true-strike attack if we do true-strike + wrathful smite on our turn
The second combat principle relates to the use of guided strike. As it turns out, with the addition of brutality, the best use of our guided strike is now to make sure that we land our setup attacks -- i.e., our BA war priest attacks or our action surge attacks -- versus using guided strike to ensure we land the true-strike attacks themselves. This might seem a bit counterintuitive, given that our true-strike attacks are our main source of damage, but the reason is that using guided strike on our setup attacks improves our damage potential by both increasing the chance that we deal damage with our setup attacks *and* by increasing the chance to hit/crit on subsequent true strike attacks by applying vex. Even though the increased chance to hit from advantage (+4.5) is smaller than that conveyed by guided strike (+10), we gain more expected damage overall if we use guided strike to make sure that we land setup attacks since this increases expected damage from setup attacks *and* expected damage from subsequent true-strike attacks through vex. For this reason, we should follow the following paradigm regarding the use of guided strike: 
  - if we miss with a BA war priest or action surge setup attack, then we should use guided strike if it will turn the miss into a hit
  - if we are doing true-strike + BA::wrathful-smite and we miss with the initial true strike attack, then use guided strike if it will turn the miss into a hit 
Finally, let's talk about how we utilize our limited resources throughout the day. Ok, so we have a max of 3 war priest charges and 4 uses of brutality. We also have a pact magic spell slot and 1 charge of action surge. All of these resources recover on a SR. Our current assumption is that we cast prayer of healing after combat 1, and then take a short rest after combat 3. So for combats 1 and 4 we will have all our uses of war priest and brutality, our action surge, and our pact magic slot available to us. If we can, we should use them all during each of these combats since we'll recover them immediately afterwards. This means, our ideal action economy in combats 1 and 4 is very deterministic. Specifically we do: 
  - turn 1: action-surge::attack_{brutality::bluff} + action::true-strike + BA::wrathful-smite 
  - turns 2, 3, and 4: BA::war-priest_{brutality::bluff} + action::true-strike 
  - we assume 1 AoO per combat and that we use brutality::bluff on hit
Note that if we hit with everything, this action plan would result in us using brutality::bluff 5 times, which is more charges than we have available. But with about an 80% chance to hit, we miss with about 1 out of every 5 attacks. And when we do miss with 1 setup attack, then we will have exactly the number of brutality charges we need. Now, as we discussed above, the chances of us missing with setup attacks are also reduced by our use of guided strike, which increases the chances that we hit the constraint on uses of brutality::bluff. But I think this is the best we can do. Next up, channel divinity. We start with 2 channel divinity charges, and gain 1 additional one on a SR. Since we only get 1 (rather than all) CD charges back on a SR, combat 1 and combat 4 are different in this regard. Specifically, we have an incentive to only use 1 CD charge in combat 1 so that we end up with 2 charges to use across combats 2 and 3. But then in combat 4, we want to use all available charges since we'll recover all of them when we long rest post-combat. That said, in expectation we'll only miss with about 1 attack per combat. So in expectation we only need to have 1 charge going in to combat 4. That means we can use both charges we have available across combats 2 and 3. So basically, the only restriction we need to code in is that we use a maximum of 1 CD charge in combat 1. Then, for all other combats we just use CD charges when we miss with setup attacks/true-strikes if we have them available. Given our resource recovery pattern, this should result in us using all 4 charges throughout the adventuring day, and having more available across combats 2/3 where we are stretching resources more thinly. Ok so then speaking of combats 2 and 3, what is our action plan in these fights? Well, basically we will have 3 war priest charges, 4 brutality charges, 6 uses of wrathful smite (including our free-cast), 1 action surge, and 2 uses of channel divinity to use across 8 combat rounds. Right so starting with war priest and action surge. These abilities both give us extra attacks, and extra attacks are more valuable (damage-wise) if we have magic weapon active. On average, we should have magic weapon active in 1 of these two-combats (though note, the way we have it coded it's randomly assigned). So the decision rule is: if we have magic weapon active then we follow the same action plan we use for combats 1 and 4. That is, use action surge + wrathful smite on turn-01, use war priest on turns 2, 3, and 4, and use brutality::bluff whenever we hit with a setup attack or attack of opportunity. And if we don't have magic weapon active, then we do true-strike + BA::wrathful-smite on every turn and don't use any brutality charges. However, to guard against the case where have zero magic weapon uptime in combats 2 and 3, we also want to state that if we start combat 3 with more than zero war priest charges, then we should revert to our combat 1/4 strategy. This just means that if we didn't have magic weapon in combat 2 and then start combat 3 and realize we don't have magic weapon again, we still use our war priest and brutality charges before they recover. On average, this should result in us using all 3 war priest charges, all 4 brutality charges, and 5 of 6 wrathful smites across these two combats. Assuming we miss w/about 1 attack per combat we will also end up using 2 guided strikes across these two combats. Perfect.
