CONCEPT NOTES:
 - Kiting playstyle where you do INT-based melee attacks and proc sneak attack and use smite
 - Precast/concentrate on alter self
 - Run up to enemy, take the attack action
 - make an unarmed strike(s) with your first/second attack and BA smite
 - if you don't have a source of advantage, use goliath hill's tumble ability to knock prone
 - then true-strike (with adv) with a rapier for your second/third attack (push, sneak attack)
 - if you don't have a source of advantage, use luck point on the rapier attack 
 - when you run out of smites, you can use poisoner with BA
 - push mastery will auto-disengage if you hit with the rapier
 - if you don't hit, defensive duelist/speedy/poisoned/frightened will keep you safe as you 
   run out of melee. 
 - With base speed (35ft) you can start/end your turn 15ft away from a creature and still 
   do all your damage. With large form, this goes up to 20ft. With longstrider this goes up 
   to 25ft. You could take speedy to boost this up to 30ft (and make enemies have disadv. on OAs)
   You could also take epic boon of speed to make kiting even more reliable. Instead, I opt 
   for charger and epic boon of combat prowess for more damage and reliability. 
 - At level-20, you're a 7th level caster (4 levels from EK, 3 from AT), meaning you would 
   have spell slots: 4-3-3-1, and you could cast 2nd level spells only. With a duration of 1hr,
   7 slots available for casting alter self should be enough to get you through any adventuring
   day. This gives you a couple high-level smites per day, and a number of low-level smites 
   (plus you have poisoner for non-spell based BA damage). 

THEMATIC CONCEPT NOTES:

 - dark/shadowfell aesthetic on a lithe, snake-like/poison themed goliath 
 - snake-like is a good theme -- fast and lethal, poisonous, but built on strong goliath frame

Level-14: Fighter-09 (Eldritch Knight), Rogue-05 (Arcane Trickster)  
 - sneak attack (3d6)

 - cunning strike

NOTE: Sneak attack damage improves and we get uncanny dodge and cunning strike. Cunning strike primarily offers additional flexibility and control. You could use the poison option in conjunction with poisoner feat to make a target make *two* CON saves or be poisoned (DC-18, DC-16). Or you could use the withdraw option if you're attacking a Huge enemy / creature with reach attacks or need the extra movement to get back to safety. Either way, nice to have the flexibility. Trip could also be useful to make an enemy have to stand up and use half move speed. Obviously it's a bit redundant with our hill's tumble ability. But even though the DC is lower, trip is also nice because it gives us an option to target DEX saves. This means we have control options to target WIS (wrathful smite/THL/sleep), DEX (trip), and CON (poisoner/poison/blindness-deafness) saves. Uncanny dodge is great for ranged attack rolls when you don't want to (or can't) cast shield. Or when melee attacks would hit you even despite defensive duelist. More abilities to protect your concentration on alter self. As a general go-to, I would probably just take the damage and not use cunning strikes. But the flexibility/additional control is nice. Especially with poison + poisoner, since that can be a really strong debuff.

NOTE: Ok classic late-build conundrum - what feat to take here? The options are speedy, charger, medium armor master, and resilient (DEX). Charger would give you more damage and a little more utility. Med. armor master and resilient are defensive (and resilient is probably better). But I think speedy is the most consistent with the identify of this character. It boosts our base movement speed to 45 and we can further boost this with longstrider (+10), jump (+20) and Large form (+10) for a max of up to 85ft. Having more speed enables this character to swoop in, attack and deal damage/control effects, and then effectively disengage and retreat to safety. I like the idea of using our additional mobility to stay out of danger, versus just having better defensive stats. Charger is perhaps more interesting. It gives us an extra 1d8 in damage per round, and it allows us to push without using push mastery. This means we could use the vex mastery on our rapier as a source of advantage, limiting our dependence on hill's tumble/luck points. But at this level, we have 12 luck points/use's of hill's tumble per day, and we can of course still use spells/traditional sources of advantage. I think another reason that I like speedy is that it means we can be more discerning in choosing our targets, potentially opting in to having OAs taken on us in order to get to an important target. Moving on, we also get a PB increase, cantrip upgrade, and we get to learn another wizard spell. I'm taking hold person here. It's less general purpose than web, but upcasts well and the potential damage increase on auto-crits is pretty huge (notably, we can load smite and sneak attack into a single true-strike attack, all of which would be doubled). This gives us a pretty lethal combo. Of note, paralyzed creatures also auto-fail DEX saves, so we can auto-prone with our trip cunning strike if we want. For a quick damage check, our basic attack combo with poisoner now does: 
 	1d6(unarmed)+1d8(rapier)+3d6(true-strike)+2d8(poisoner)+4d6(sneak-attack)+2INT = 8d6+3d8+10 = 51.5 damage and poisons
And the lvl-1 wrathful smite combo does: 
 	1d6(unarmed)+1d6(wrathful-smite)+1d8(rapier)+3d6(true-strike)+4d6(sneak-attack)+2INT = 9d6+1d8+10 = 46 damage and fears
And our max damage combo with both poison and smite does: 
	1d6(unarmed)+1d8(rapier)+3d6(wrathful-smite)+2d8(poison)+3d6(true-strike)+4d6(sneak-attack)+2INT = 11d6+3d8+10 = 62 damage
And both poisons and frightens. Decent damage with good control for this level. And if we managed to get someone with hold person on turn-1, then got them with poisoner and a lvl-3 smite on turn-2 we would do: 
        2d6(unarmed)+2d8(rapier)+6d6(wrathful-smite)+2d8(poison)+6d6(true-strike)+8d6(sneak-attack)+2INT = 4d8+20d6 = 95 damage
