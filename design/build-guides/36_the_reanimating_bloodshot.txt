MECHANICAL CONCEPT NOTES
 - start out as a true-strike blood hunter wielding a pistol; assume we get weapon masteries from blood.hunter 
 - leverage archery fighting style, vex mastery, and repeating shot (+1) for high chance to hit even as we delay boosting INT
 - true-strike + crimson rite offsets damage from not getting extra attack and delaying INT increases 
 - once you get reanimated companion, use dreadful swipe as primary bonus action for damage 
 - you can use atmokinesis (shocking grasp, convert pistol shots to lightning dmg) in order to keep it alive 
 - you can also use true-strike to shoot it and trigger death burst 
 - take poisoner for BA dmg when companion isn't around; don't need war caster since we won't be concentrating on anything important 
   and we won't ever need to cast spells (other than true strike) in combat. For true strike, the pistol is our spellcasting focus + the 
   material component for the spell, so we can still cast it while using a shield 
 - profane soul gives us pact magic slots that we can use to resummon our reanimated companion (should use all slots on this); w/3 lvl-01 
   slots (one of which recovers on a SR), we can create up to 5 reanimated companions per day. This should be enough for all combats + 
   some wiggle room. 
 - boost defense through +1 shield, defensive fighting style (fighter), and healing from rite focus of undying patron
 - we go rock gnome for a few reasons - first we want adv on mental saves since our WIS and CHA are bad; second rock gnome clockwork tools 
   synergize with the oil/flammable materials we can create with alchemists tools, which we get from blood.hunter. We can also use igniter + 
   oil to blow up our reanimated companion, making for some great trap potential 
 - eventually we go arcane archer to get bursting shot and piercing shot; bursting shot lets us dmg our reanimated companion, potentially 
   triggering death burst, while still making true-strike attacks against our main target. Piercing shot lets us shoot our companion and 
   our primary target through full cover. atmokinesis let's us heal our companion while still dealing dmg to our primary target if we want 
 - shocking grasp, dread swipe and tactical shift all give us a way to disengage if we get stuck in melee 
 - conceptually, at early levels the reanimated companion is durable enough to be effective in combat, at later levels we just want to send 
   him in and then blow him up to deal AOE damage
 - post lvl-11, when we get extra attack, we move towards building DEX in order to gain damage by making multiple attacks per turn
 - we take mage slayer to further boost our mental save resilience, and sharpshooter to increase our effective range out to 90ft 
 - we finish out profane soul in order to get some more spellcasting, mystic frenzy (which we could use to do true-strike + pistol attack) 
   and epic boon of fate for more saving throw bonuses at lvl-20

Ok, let's just start with the PB and cantrip scaling. With a +3 PB, we now have a +9 to hit with our pistol (+3 INT, +3 PB, +2 archery, +1 repeating shot), meaning we have a base 75% chance to hit, and a 93.75% chance to hit with advantage. So our chance to hit increases. We also get to add true strike damage (+3.5) on hit. So now our DPR if we just focused on true-strike attacks would be: 
  (attacking w/advantage): (0.75)*(0.9375)*(15.5) 
  (attacking w/o advantage): (0.25)*(0.75)*(15.5)

Next let's talk about the reanimated companion. The first obvious point is that the reanimated companion gives us a way to deal damage with our BA. Assuming we are able to use our BA to attack with the companion 75% of the time (with a 60% chance to hit), this adds:  
  (dreadful swipe): (0.75)*(0.6)*(7.5) = 3.375 dmg 
The companion also deals damage when it dies. Assuming we use all our spell slots to summon reanimated companions each adventuring day, we will get 3 death bursts. At lvl-05, monsters have an average DEX save of +2. Our spell DC is 14. So there's a 55% chance they fail. On failure, enemies take 2d6 necrotic damage. Ideally, we are always hitting multiple targets with this. But let's assume we get 1.5 enemies in the radius on average. Given these assumptions, over 16 combat rounds, the companion's death burst feature adds: 
  (death burst): 3*1.5*(0.55)*(7)/16 = 1.08 dmg 
In addition, we now have a concrete use for our blood curse of the fallen puppet. Namely, we should use it after our reanimated companion dies to give immediately allow it to attack (using dreadful swipe). Assuming we do this twice per day (1/8 of combat rounds), and that we amplify it each time (which gives our dead companion an additional +INT to the attack, bringing the total to +9 to hit) we get an additional: 
  (fallen puppet): (1/8)*(0.75)*(7.5) = 0.70 dmg 
Considering all these different damage sources, our overall DPR comes to: 
  (total): 18.96 DPR 
So that's a 84% increase in damage relative to lvl-04, and is pretty decent damage for the start of tier-02. By comparison, a fighter with STR=18 (65% chance to hit) and the great weapon master feat would be doing: 2*(0.65)*(13.5) = 17.55 DPR, though they would get more from their subclass and the BA hew attack they get on a crit. Still, not to shabby. 

Ok now lets talk about the other build synergies with the reanimated companion. First off, we have the atmokinesis feat. This gives us shocking grasp, which we can use out of combat to heal our companion with zero resources. We can also use our action to shoot our companion and use the lightning jolt feature of the atmokinesis feat to convert our piercing damage to lightning damage. Doing this would heal the companion for an average of 12hp. Next, we can cast fog cloud, then use our BA to command our companion to attack an enemy within the cloud. Because the companion has blindsight, they will have advantage on the attack roll and blinded enemies will have disadvantage on attacks against them. There is also synergy with gust of wind. Namely we can get the wind blowing behind our companion, potentially pushing them 15ft further towards our intended target, while simultaneously keeping enemies at bay (halves speed against wind). Neither of these spell synergies is hugely impactful, and I probably wouldn't sacrifice making attacks to cast them, but situationally they could be the right answer for boosting the damage/durability of our companion (especially if we can pre-cast). Next up, we have our item synergies. In particular, our reanimated companion can carry our fire starter clockwork devices and activate them with its bonus action (without interfering with its ability to attack). Set up cool traps with this, where you throw/pour oil and then ignite it with the companion, potentially triggering its death burst. The companion can also do things like place hunting traps, drop caltrops, or use manacles. All of these would require that we use our BA to command the companion to take the utilize action. Finally, hitting enemies with dreadful swipe negates enemies abilities to take reactions, which could be especially helpful for us if we want to disengage from a melee attacker. 

Whew, ok now the next reanimator feature we get is jolt to life. Ideally we aren't using our action on our turn to cast a healing cantrip, but obviously situations arise where allies are downed. This feature not only means we get a resource-free 1HP restoration 3x/day, but also makes it so that when we are reviving allies in combat, we still are dealing a bit of damage. It's also super nice that this comes online right when we get cantrip scaling, so that we can do it from up to 30ft away. Finally, we get new spells. Right so final thoughts on this build. Damage wise, we basically keep pace with the Treantmonk blade-pact greatsword warlock at every level. Similar to this baseline build, we are in the `C' class of damage in every tier (tier-01, tier-02, tier-03, and tier-04). In fact, across all 20 levels, our average DPR is 98% of the baseline, so almost exactly the same. But we actually do a bit better in tier-03 (104% of baseline) where many campaigns see the most play. Mechanically, we get to do a bunch of fun/cool stuff by managing having our little grenade reanimated companion and having a lot of interesting cantrips/abilities to play with. And we have a very very cohesive and thematic character identity through all 20 levels. We also get sizeable jumps in damage right at the tier breakpoints (+84% at lvl-05, +26% at lvl-11, and +9% at lvl-17) which should make our progression seem compatible with most adventures. We also maintain this damage profile while playing a ranged character (though only at 30ft for much of our career), with very high AC and lots of HP (certainly relative to other ranged builds) and pretty excellent defensive features (gnome cunning, mage slayer, built in healing through rite focus and second wind, damage redirection to summons, boon of fate) and a bit of control (fog-cloud, gust of wind, poisoner). Plus we get to add a bunch of utility through a great set of skill and tool proficiencies, item crafting, summons, zero-cost revives (jolt to life), and ritual spell casting. In addition, this build is cool because it relies on a bunch of different sources of damage, and the flexibility with which we can dish out damage increases as we progress (for example, in the multiple options we end up having for dealing damage with our BA). So we don't end up just having one thing that we do and if that doesn't work we're screwed. This flexibility also includes the fact that we have good ways to deal both multi- and single-target damage, which is also really valuable. It's also one of the few builds I've ever done that starts out as a true-strike build, moves into making multiple attacks with extra attack, and then moves *back* to true strike to round things out when we get mystic frenzy (and where that transition actually smoothly increases DPR). All-in-all, pretty neat idea. Glad that it worked out to be so fun, flexible, and thematically consistent while being competitive damage wise at every level. Can't really ask for much more. 10/10 would play.

Mechanical Concept 
 - start out as a true-strike blood hunter wielding a pistol; assume we get weapon masteries from blood.hunter 
 - leverage archery fighting style, vex mastery, and repeating shot (+1) for high chance to hit even as we delay boosting INT
 - true-strike + crimson rite offsets damage from not getting extra attack and delaying INT increases 
 - once you get reanimated companion, use dreadful swipe as primary bonus action for damage 
 - you can use atmokinesis (shocking grasp, convert pistol shots to lightning dmg) in order to keep it alive 
 - you can also use true-strike to shoot it and trigger death burst 
 - take ritual caster in order to get utility spells; don't need war caster since we won't be concentrating on anything important 
   and we won't ever need to cast spells (other than true strike) in combat. For true strike, the pistol is our spellcasting focus + the 
   material component for the spell, so we can still cast it while using a shield 
 - profane soul gives us pact magic slots that we can use to resummon our reanimated companion (should use all slots on this); w/3 lvl-01 
   slots (one of which recovers on a SR), we can create up to 5 reanimated companions per day. This should be enough for all combats + 
   some wiggle room. 
 - boost defense through +1 shield, defensive fighting style (fighter), and healing from rite focus of undying patron
 - we go rock gnome for a few reasons - first we want adv on mental saves since our WIS and CHA are bad; second rock gnome clockwork tools 
   synergize with the oil/flammable materials we can create with alchemists tools, which we get from blood.hunter. We can also use igniter + 
   oil to blow up our reanimated companion, making for some great trap potential 
 - eventually we go arcane archer to get bursting shot and piercing shot; bursting shot lets us dmg our reanimated companion, potentially 
   triggering death burst, while still making true-strike attacks against our main target. Piercing shot lets us shoot our companion and 
   our primary target through full cover (use a sight-through-familiar to do this fully remotely). atmokinesis let's us heal our companion 
   while still dealing dmg to our primary target if we want 
 - dread swipe and tactical shift both give us a way to disengage, if we get stuck in melee 
 - conceptually, at early levels the reanimated companion is durable enough to be effective in combat, at later levels we just want to send 
   him in and then blow him up to deal AOE damage
 - post lvl-11, when we get extra attack, we move towards building DEX in order to gain damage by making multiple attacks per turn
 - we take mage slayer to further boost our mental save resilience, and sharpshooter to increase our effective range out to 90ft 
 - we finish out profane soul in order to get some more spellcasting, mystic frenzy (which we could use to do true-strike + pistol attack) 
   and epic boon of fate for more saving throw bonuses at lvl-20 

Thematic Concept 
 - we are a blood.hunter with a lich-like patron who controls undead to do their bidding, so we do the same and create an undead minion
   which we use send in and mercilessly destroy to smite our enemies 
 - just as we are a monster to fight monsters, so too do we control the undead to fight our enemies 
 - spooky necromancer/blood.hunter/dark-magic arcane archer
 - we would be a total social pariah, an outcast among outcasts and a definitely a creepy dude 
 - make reanimated companion a creepy tim-burton like creature -- addam's family `thing', undead doggo, undead rat-thing (skaven) 
   make it something you don't mind blowing up 


At lvl-20: 
 - move reanimated companion over towards group of enemies (no action required) 
 - true-strike w/pistol deals: 1d10+3d6(TS)+1d6(rite)+5(INT) = 24.5 dmg 
 - then use busting shot to deal an additional: 2d6 (7) force dmg 
 - assume AOE kills our reanimated companion, whose death burst deals another 2d6 (7) necrotic dmg 
 - use our reaction to do blood curse of the fallen puppet, allowing our companion to dreadful strike for: 1d4+7 (9.5) dmg
 - then using mystic frenzy, we make another pistol attack with our BA, which deals: 1d10 + 1d6(rite) + 5(DEX) (14) dmg
 - for a total of: 62 dmg 

At lvl-20 (Alternatively) 
 - move reanimated companion over towards group of enemies (no action required)
 - BA: command companion to dreadful strike: 1d4 + 7 (9.5) dmg
 - attack w/pistol: 1d10 + 1d6(rite) + 5(DEX) (14) dmg
 - bursting shot: 2d6 (7) dmg
 - death burst: 2d6 (7) dmg 
 - blood curse of fallen puppet: 1d4+7 (9.5) dmg
 - second attack: 1d10 + 1d6(rite) + 5(DEX) (14) dmg
 - total = 28 (pistol attacks) + 14 (bursts) + 19 (swipes) = 61 dmg
