MECHANICAL CONCEPT NOTES
 - Primordial warlock signature feature (elemental node) gives you a way to use pact magic slots while raging for extra damage
 - Core concept is to use weapon mastery (topple), feats (slasher, cold caster), and species/class features (frost's chill, brutal strike) to slow 
   enemies, trapping them in/near elemental node and keeping them aggro'd on us 
 - Ancestral barbarian gives you spiritual protectors feature, enabling extra cold damage on weapon attacks; combine this with the cold-caster feat, 
   which reduces subsequent saving throws when you deal cold damage --> more reliable topples/elemental node damage 
 - Stack multiple sources of movement speed reduction: use battleaxe (slashing, topple), slasher feat, frost's chill, hamstring blow. If everything procs, 
   we'll be able to inflict prone and reduce movement speed by 35ft. For most creatures, this will immobilize them completely ("freeze" them)  
 - While less effective against enemies who can teleport, the nice thing about movement speed reduction is that it's very generalizeable, and 
   essentially never countered.  
 - In general, use warlock invocations and warlock/elemental spells for defense and out-of-combat utility/support and use barbarian class/subclass
   features for damage. 
 - Go frost goliath to get additional slow, plus additional movement speed from large form; elemental spells gives us darkvision when we need it 
 - At character creation, only go 12 points into CON since we won't depend on concentration, then get tough feat via lessons of the first ones. 
   Between that and barbarian hit die, we should be quite tanky. 
 - Start warlock-01 to get WIS/CHA save proficiency, relying on rage + high-STR for STR saves, and don't worry about CON saves since we aren't 
   concentrating in combat; eventually get lucky via lessons of the first ones to further protect (mental) saves; use armor of shadows + shield for 
   AC (at least until lvl-11 when we get elemental haven) since going warlock-01 means we lose out on medium armor proficiency
 - Lvl-06 subclass features are primarily defensive, making us an effective tank and support. At barbarian-06, we get spirit shield, reducing damage 
   taken by allies and at warlock-06, elemental haven gives us non-magical teleports and a large boost to our AC (+CHA)
 - Starting in tier-03 all the way through tier-04, we go full barbarian to get additional support/mobility/damage features, including 
   brutal strike (hamstring-blow) for more damage and slow. 
 - B/c barb damage falls off at higher levels, in late game we are more of a tank/controller. But going full barb starting at lvl-12 gets us 
   the vengeful spirits sublcass feature at lvl-20, which represents a very nice boost to damage. 
 - As with many martial builds, you would probably need a magic weapon in order to stay top tier for damage through late game. Also worth noting 
   that we will be pretty reliant on cold damage for this build, and will be significantly less effective against enemies with cold resistance/immunity 
   (this too too common, however -- only ~ 19% of monsters in monster manual or cold resistant/immune). That said, we do have flexibility when 
   it comes to the damage dealt by spiritual protectors. 
 - Finally, also worth noting that this character would really shine in a campaign setting with significant interaction with aquatic environments

   * spirits can cause one of 3 effects: 
   * (i) Distract: until start of next turn, target has Disadv on attack rolls against creatures other than you 
   * (ii) Protect: until end of target's next turn, next time it hits a creature other than you w/attack roll, creature has resistance 
   * (iii) Strike: target takes an extra 1d6 damage (acid, cold, fire, force, lightning, or thunder) 
 
NOTE: Alright, big level here. Lvl-05 and we go barb-03 to get our subclass - path of the ancestral guardian. We also get a proficiency bonus increase, an additional use of rage, primal knowledge and we get access to the large form feature of our goliath species. The big thing here is our signature subclass feature: spiritual protectors. Thematically, I imagine these as `elemental protectors' manifesting in a spray of spectral/ethereal waves that dance around us, arc off our weapon attacks, and coalesce into sheets/spikes of ice in order to protect and deal damage. Think of this power as manifesting from an emergent connection to our sea-giant ancestry. Now, we get three options - distract, protect, and strike. At this stage in our career, we should feel free to use any. Distract creates a soft taunt against a target that is augmented by our use of reckless attacks (a target would have advantage against us, and disadvantage against all others). Protect grants damage reduction to allies. And strike gives us extra damage. FWIW, in a few levels we will be shifting to using the `strike' option almost exclusively, so it may feel more natural to just start doing that now. But then again, it's nice to have a bit of strategic decision making to do in an otherwise simple `hit things with axe' build. Note, we can stack the strike option with frost's chill (which we can now use x3 times per LR) to approximate a `smite' effect, dealing an additional 2d6 on hit. I imagine this as our weapon trailing spectral water that coalesces around our target just as we hit it, then freezes and explodes, sending ice shards into our enemy. Very cool. Distract + large form is also a decent option, as we can use our large size to block choke points, extend our effective reach, and draw attention away from our allies. The extra speed from large form also makes us more effective at getting into melee with key targets. Next up, getting another use of rage means that we now have effectively 4 uses of rage per LR, so we can use it in every one of our assumed 4 daily combats. Alternatively, we now have additional uses to spend on primal knowledge. Speaking of, primal knowledge is a nice out-of-combat utility feature -- giving us the option to expend uses of rage to help us succeed on key ability checks. In terms of DPR, we see gains from our proficiency bonus increase (increasing our chance to hit and uses of frost's chill) and, of course, from our persistent use of the strike option of spiritual protectors. Given conservative assumptions, our DPR probably levels out around 15-16, which is at the bottom of the barrel in D-class for tier-02. But it's early days, and we did see nice increases in defensive and out-of-combat utility. So that's good.

NOTE: Lvl-06 and we go barbarian-04 to get an ASI/Feat. We take the slasher feat, which allows us to increase our STR to 18 and gives us two important features. First, we get the hamstring feature which allows us to impose a 10ft slow when we deal slashing damage with attacks. Now when we hit with our battleaxe we can inflict a 10ft slow and potentially topple, which would further halve the opponents' speed. On it's own I don't think hamstring effect is that great, but when combined with topple weapon mastery, it makes our basic attacks impart a significant degree of battlefield control. In combination with our frost's chill species feature, we can really lock down an enemy (-20ft + prone), making us an effective tank. More specifically, for creatures with base speed of 30ft, if we reduce their speed by 20ft and topple them, they will only have 5ft of movement left once they stand up. In this setting, it might even make sense to move back from the enemy after attacking them (ideally burning their reaction to make an AoO and potentially procing armor of agathys for more damage) so that even after they stand up we are more than 5ft away from them and they can't even reach us to attack us on their turn. But hamstring isn't the only thing we get from slasher, we also get the enhanced critical feature -- which makes it so when we crit with slashing damage, the enemy has disadvantage on attack rolls until the start of our next turn. At the very least, this can help offset the advantage we will give our enemies when we make reckless attacks. And crits are more likely if we're making (reckless) attacks with advantage. So that's nice. More generously, the enhanced critical helps establish our identity as a controller and tank, as we can lock down and debuff our targets. Oh and also, at barbarian-04 we also get an additional weapon mastery. Here I think we take whip mastery, which is a slashing weapon and has the slow mastery -- meaning for us it slows by 20ft on hit -- which is pretty good. Plus we'll have more uses for the whip in a few levels. Ok, so overall, boosting our STR increases our damage a bit, but we're still not doing a ton. So lean in to the tank/controller identity.

Also unlike flaming sphere, it's location isn't limited by physics/the environment and it can be placed/move anywhere (i.e. it can float in the air if we want it to). So when we summon the node, and when enemies enter the node's space or end their turn within it, they are forced to make a DEX save or take damage. Now it's only a piddling 1d6, but since we can move the sphere, it's definitely possible for us to tag multiple targets with it. Furthermore, with our capacity for movement speed reduction, we can also potentially `trap' or `freeze' enemies within the sphere, making them take its damage multiple times. This is the core concept of this build, the reason why we wanted to go barbarian/frost goliath/battleaxe for speed reduction synergy. Basic concept is that now on turn-01 of combat, we use our BA to rage, then we use a magic action to summon our elemental node. Then on subsequent turns, we use our action to attack, slowing and toppling enemies, and use our BA to move the sphere and deal damage. Ideally, on each turn we can move the sphere to tag multiple enemies, and then also `park it' on the enemy that we've toppled/slowed with our attacks, making it likely that they will take the damage on their own turn or, at the very least, that we'll be able to move the sphere to tag them with it again on our next turn. Also note since the node does force a saving throw, we can use it to extend our rage if for some reason we aren't able to make attacks on our turn. Ok, so in terms of usage, we basically get two `free' uses of this per day (assuming 1 SR) but will otherwise have to use pact magic slots to summon the node again. On average, this means that we basically use 1 pact slot per short rest to summon our node (assuming we SR after combat 2), leaving us with only 1 other pact magic slot for casting warlock spells. Of course, we also have magical cunning. But it's probably a good idea to use 1 pact slots at the start of each adventuring day to cast darkvision on ourselves (one of our primordial spells), and then immediately use magical cunning to restore the slot. This means we'll really only be able to cast armor of agathys on ourselves at most twice per day. Bummer. But, since our pact slots leveled up, at least when we do we are now getting 10 THP (effectively 20 while we rage), so there's some chance we can stretch it for the full duration and we deal more damage when enemies hit us. And of course, if we're in a setting where darkvision isn't going to be useful, then save magical cunning and cast armor of agathys more often (or save the slot for other utility spells). Right so then in terms of spellcasting, we get some bound spells from the primordial subclass. Specifically, we get chromatic orb and ice-knife as lvl-01 spells. Probably won't ever use these, but I guess it is nice to have a bit of flexibility for ranged attack options. And ice-knife is very thematic. For lvl-02 spells we get darkvision and alter slef. Darkvision is great, since we don't have it naturally as a goliath. Alter self is somewhat situational, but would let us give ourselves water breathing and a swim speed when we need to explore aquatic environments. This let's us lean more heavily into the RP of our sea giant ancestry and power. We could also use alter self to disguise ourselves to facilitate infiltration/reconnaissance. We do have decent stealth, and proficiency in all CHA-based social skills, so this could be useful. But for both the aquatic adaptation and change appearance options, I would really only consider using them in exploration settings when it's unlikely that we'll be getting into combat. But still, fun to get some additional versatility and out of combat utility. Next, we get one additional warlock spell preparation. Given that the demand for our spell slots is already exceedingly high, and that we can't cast spells in combat while raging, we just want to pick something for situational out of combat utility. I went with spider climb, which would allow us to gain a climb speed when we need it. In my imagination, I envision the spell working for this character by creating binding frost/ice that `sticks' our feet to ceilings/vertical surfaces while it's active. Again really would only use this for exploration/out-of-combat purposes, but it is nice to be a barbarian that actually does have some out-of-combat utility. The other main alternative consideration here was invisibility (though mirror image and misty step are honorable mentions). But in the end, I like the parallel of having alter self for aquatic environments and spider climb for climbing scenarios. And, as an `elemental' barbarian, it feels more consistent/thematic to have our elemental spells work to enhance/augment our physical prowess and capabilities (vs our stealth). Ok yeah, so in terms of DPR, we probably stay about even. While active, we effectively add a little more than +1d6 DPR through elemental node, assuming that we deal damage to at least creature per turn (probably more given that it can be used in AOE, but they might save..). Furthermore, the node can also deal additional damage if our allies force-move enemies into it on their turns, which is increasingly likely given that we're slowing targets and keeping them near the node. We probably also deal slightly more damage through using armor of agathys with lvl-02 pact magic slots, even though we use it less often. On the other hand, our DPR definitely takes a significant hit because using our action to summon the elemental node means we are losing out on attacks on our first turn of combat. Now, we should definitely pre-cast it when we can, but with a duration of 1m this won't be all that easy to do. So yeah, probably still somewhere in the 25-30 DPR range, which is just fine. Overall, the playstyle with elemental node active is just so much more fun, strategic, and thematic. Right and then finally just for a short note on our subclass themes. This type of patron would provide a thematic through line connecting our species (goliath), barbarian subclass (ancestral spirits of water/sea giants) and our warlock subclass (primordial, elemental plane of water). And the discovery of our connection to this ancestry and patron would provide an excellent basis for a character backstory and personal story quest. Ok, whew! Long note. Moving on.

Level-15: Warlock-06 (Primordial), Barbarian-09 (Ancestral Guardian) 
 - rage (4, +3) 
 - brutal strike (x1/turn) 
   * when you make a STR-based attack w/advantage, can forgo the advantage to impart additional effects on hit
   * these on hit effects include +1d10 damage and either: 
     - (i) forceful blow = push target 15ft and move up to half speed w/o provoking AoOs
     - (ii) hamstring blow = target's speed is reduced by 15ft until start of your next turn 
 
NOTE: Ok lvl-15 and we go barbarian-09. Couple of nice things here. First off, our rage bonus increases to +3, which increases our damage and also increases the amount of damage that we can reduce with our elemental shield (3d6 = 10.5). Sweet. We also get the brutal strike barbarian base class feature. And this is soo good for us. So as a reminder, the way brutal strike works is that when we make a STR-based attack with advantage (for example, if we opt to use reckless attack on our turn) then we can forgo the advantage on one of our attacks in order to impart additional effects on hit. The first of these effects is extra damage. Specifically, when we hit with a brutal strike, we deal an additional 1d10 (5.5) weapon damage. Then, we also get to choose whether we want to (i) push the target 15ft away and move up to half our speed towards them (forceful blow) or (ii) reduce their movement speed by 15ft until the start of our next turn. Now forceful blow is a good option. And we should use it when appropriate. But, in case it wasn't obvious, we are most interested in the hamstring blow option here. And, in case it wasn't obvious, we should try to do this on every turn possible, including using reckless attacks when necessary to give ourselves advantage. With hamstring blow and slasher, we can now reduce enemy movement speed by 25ft on hit. Every. Turn. If we tack on frost's chill, we can reduce movement speed by 35ft. And if we make the attack with our whip, we can reduce movement speed by up to 45ft. And of course, if we're attacking with our battleaxe (our default) we are still forcing a penalized (-1d4) DC-18 CON save or the enemy is knocked prone (penalized b/c of cold-caster + elemental protectors). For a creature with a 30ft of movement, if we reduce their movement speed by 25 (to 5) and knock them prone (forcing them to use half their speed to stand), they effectively won't be able to move on their turn unless they use their action to dash (and even if they do, they're only going 5ft). This means if they are within our elemental node when we knock them down, they will take the damage on our turn, and be forced to end their turn within it, taking the damage again. If we decide to use frost's chill and reduce their movement speed by 35ft, all the way down to zero, then they won't even be able to stand up from prone on their turn. So badass. I mean just the flavor of freezing enemies in place with frost-infused strikes, trapping them in our swirling node of freezing water and ice. So cool. And this is kind of the capstone, mechanically, for this character concept -- building movement speed reduction and damage via martial feats and barbarian levels, and leveraging the primordial warlock elemental node for AOE/non-concentration damage while raging. All while providing superior battlefield control and meaningful defensive utility. In terms of DPR, we get the boost to our rage damage (+2 DPR), the extra damage from brutal strike (+5.5 DPR), and we increase the damage dealt with our elemental node by trapping enemies in it on their turn (maybe +1.5 DPR) -- all told a total increase of approximately +9 DPR. Huge jump, putting us in the 40-49 DPR range. Solidly B-class for tier-03. Perfect for our hybrid control tank/support frontliner type of character. Amazing.
