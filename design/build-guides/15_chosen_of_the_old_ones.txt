APPEARANCE 
She is a pale blue (almost white) lizardfolk female. She is tall, lithe and athletic. Her skin has dark blue and lavender accents on the scales on her forehead, throat, shoulders, tail, and ankles. She has a bright crimson crest of braided and beaded hair running from the top of her head all the way to the midnight blue tip of her 4ft long whip-like tail. She has delicate features, including a soft face that protrudes onto a rounded tapering snout, slender dexterous hands tipped with black claws, and long almost birdlike feet ending in talons. Her eyes are those of a lizard, with black vertical slit pupils and massive yellow irises flecked with gold and ringed in vibrant violet. She wears a touch of eyeshadow to give her eyes a bit of a smokey look. She has a few small gold rings in one eyebrow, and wears a few small gold rings set with stones on the fingers of her left hand. One heavy gold-link chain lies draped around her neck. Geometric-pattern tattoos run in bands around her wrists, ankles, and at the end of her tail. On her upper body, she wears an oxblood-colored sleeveless silk shirt that folds across her chest and tucks into sturdy leather wraps that are tied about her waist. The shirt is decorated with a bit of embroidery in thread-of-gold, depicting birds in flight, running across the chest. On her head, she wears a loosely-wrapped headscarf that is the same color as her shirt. The scarf is heavily embroidered with gold birds. On her lower body, she wears tight-fitting mid-calf length, oxblood-colored breeches. Her lower calves are covered with leather wrappings. Her ankles and bare, clawed feet are exposed. She wears a long-bladed dagger in a gator-skin sheath strapped to her left thigh. At her left waist, she carries a long narrow-bladed rapier in a cherry wood scabbard. Both the scabbard and the hilt of the sword are lightly gilded. On her right hip is a quiver of arrows, and she carries a dark wood longbow in her right hand. On her back, she wears an adventurer's backpack. Strapped to the back of the pack is a large, circular, tortoise-shell shield. 

PERSONALITY/MANNERISMS 
 - in the quick twitchy way she looks about you might see intensity, curiosity and intelligence; 
   others might see the frightened, hungry eyes of a beast 
 - she is wary of humans and clearly unfamiliar with the quotidian environs of towns and 
   cities, but brims with an excited curiosity to see and learn how things work. 
 - earnest and quick of tongue, she is as likely to offer a playful barb as to make 
   a fool of herself by speaking out of her depth 
 - she sort of squawks softly in surprise (like a chicken clucking) if she is started 
   or if you say something intriguing or disconcerting

BACKSTORY NOTES 
 - chosen of the impoverished, downtrodden, waning old-one lizardfolk tribes 
 - the blood of the old ones is thinning (think 'we are the last of the Amichi'), 
   and the great old saurians struggle to hold together an ancient society as lizardmen
   continue to transform/descend into beasts
 - the blood of the old ones is cursed/poisoned; despite our hero's athleticism and
   physical prowess, the cursed constitution of the old ones weakens the body, 
   and eventually drives one to madness
 - our hero is adventuring to find a way to lift the curse/prevent the decline
   of her people, and to restore glory and honor to the name of the old ones
 - trained in war and ancient druidic magic by the old sages and bastions of 
   her home temple-city
 - the lizardmen martial and magic traditions focus on the preservation of life 
   and nature, and the extermination and abjuration of the wicked 
 - our hero has always been exceptional among her people; at times she has been 
   called a prodigy; she looks down on her lesser and she struggles to accept the 
   `base' and `animalistic' traditions define her kin, and which she herself 
   sometimes desires to embrace. 
 - as she progresses in levels, I imagine this character moving towards some kind of
   `enlightenment' about the role/fate of the lizardfolk and how their collective story
   is related to the gods, and the (tragic) past/future of the world in which they live. 
 - perhaps her narrative has a kind of `last of the great saurians' vibe, in she 
   helps to steward in a new age of humanity/gods in which her people -- whose purpose 
   no longer exists, and whose role in the making/securing of the modern world is 
   defunct -- gracefully exit stage left and become fully a part of nature (i.e., 
   turn into beasts)

NOTE: Alright, this might seem like a strange choice. Why the skill expert feat? The main reason is that we want to get to 14 CHA so that we have a +2 modifier when we take paladin-6 and get aura of protection next level. Other half feats that give +1 CHA aren't very thematic (actor, fey touched, etc.). Shield master (SM) is also a good option for us (and thematic), but mechanically it's weaker than skill expert. SM gives us evasion and a +2/+3 (depending on our shield) to DEX saves, but increasing our CHA will give us and our allies (including our mount) a +2 to *all* saving throws next level. With our low STR we also can't really take advantage of the BA shove that SM gives us. By contrast, since we also increase our PB at level-9, taking skill expert immediately gives us a big bump to some important skills (+10 perception, +6 to insight). Becoming more insightful and perceptive is also more consistent with taking star druid levels (more prophetic). At druid-4 we also get access to CR 1/2 wild shape forms, and we learn another form. I'd probably keep giant lizard as a mount/climbing option and keep spider for stealth. Take reef shark so you have a pure aquatic option, then switch out velociraptor for clawfoot raptor, which would be your go-to combat form. Probably you aren't using wildshape that often, but this set gives you a lot of versatility while still being lizard-y. Crocodile is another good option, but is largely redundant with your natural lizardfolk abilities. 

Another game changer is the 4th level spell slot we get. While we could use this for a big smite or casting aid for more hit points, probably the best thing to do is to use it to cast find-steed, giving us a flying mount. In addition to awesome mobility for positioning, since we're a DEX-based paladin who uses a longbow, a flying mount also means we can just sit 150 up in the air and rain arrows down on fools if we want. Fuck yeah. Overall, this level isn't a big power spike combat-wise, but we get a TON of additional utility from skill expert, additional wild shape options, our flying mount, and we even get an extra cantrip. Note, the cantrips I took here are meant to be the most consistent with our paladin/druid protector/seer identity. Others such as elementalism/message might be more useful in game.

You can also haste your mount to get a 140ft fly speed. As a damage option it's only ok, since you still can only smite once per turn (only one BA). Protection from energy + absorb elements means you can 1/4 incoming elemental damage. Other useful/thematic paladin spells include: clairvoyance, beacon of hope, aura of vitality, spirit guardians. Blinding smite is also pretty good, but the DC of your spell save isn't the greatest. We also get a 5th level spell slot. Use it to upcast find steed, aid, mass healing word, or smites.

NOTE: As always, hard to decide what makes the most sense in terms of ASI/feats at the end of a characters progression. Increasing WIS increases our perception and insight, WIS save, as well as our spell modifier for our druid spells. Increasing CHA gives us another +1 to all saving throws with aura of protection and a small boost to our social abilities. Tough would increase our HP from 114 (avg) to 148 (avg). Shield master would give us evasion, a +10 to our DEX saves, and a bonus action DC-15 shove. War caster would give us advantage on concentration checks. I think (at the moment, anyway) I would probably go with shield master. We've had our shield since level 1, and DEX saves are the most common type of save in the game and typically to prevent flat damage. We'll be hard to hit with attack rolls, but even half damage on big spells can wear us down. It also has the `cool' factor, imo, over tough/ASIs. It adds a little bit more martial flavor to our gish-y character. 

In addition, at druid-8 we also learn another wildshape form, and get access to CR-1 forms. Switch out reef shark for giant octopus for your aquatic form. Switch out giant lizard for moorbounder (mighty nein creature) as a land-based mount (speed 70, great jumping ability), though maybe reflavor this to a reptilian form (e.g., cold one). Switch out clawfoot raptor for flying snake. We won't use wildshape in combat at this level anyway, and the flying snake is tiny, can fly and swim, and has blindsight. It's also very thematic. Then add the giant eagle as a large fast flying form, capable of carrying allies etc. Would probably also want to reflavor this so it's reptilian. You could switch giant wolf spider to giant spider. But the wolf spider is faster, smaller, and has bonuses to perception the big spider doesn't. It just doesn't have a web attack or as much HP, which we don't care about. 

Finally, we get a 7th level spell slot. As above, probably makes the most sense to use it to upcast aid (30 HP), find steed, healing spells or smites.
