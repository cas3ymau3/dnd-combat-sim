MECHANICAL CONCEPT NOTES
 - DEX-CHA monk with sorcerer (and warlock) multiclass; enhance monk abilities with spellcasting; use 
   high CHA and skill proficiencies to be a `face' type character with the monk (rather than sorc) chassis 
 - leverage draconic sorc subclass + tough for HP even though we sacrifice CON at point buy 
 - take cryokinesis origin feat to get armor of agathys for additional durability and damage
 - together these HP/THP boosting abilities will help us manage our relatively low AC (not terrible tho)
 - take weapon master feat early to get dagger (nick) mastery, enabling 3 attacks per turn w/o extra attack
 - go tattooed warrior monk subclass for access to blindsight; idea is to combine this with fog cloud and 
   (eventually) sleet storm to gain advantage on all our attacks while imposing disadvantage on enemy attacks
 - take sentinel feat so that we can use our reaction to lock enemies within our fog cloud/sleet storm 
 - until we get sentinel, use sorcery points to create lvl-01 spell slots for more castings of armor of agathys 
 - once we have sentinel at lvl-08, start casting fog cloud. then immediately at lvl-09 go sorc-05 to get 
   more sorcery points and use those to fuel buffs like darkvision and magic weapon. 
 - go with extended spell and careful spell for metamagic options. Use extended to give ourselves (near) full-day 
   up time on magic weapon and armor of agathys. Eventually use careful spell so that we can cast sleet storm 
   around ourselves and auto-succeed on the first DEX save (so it lasts at least 1-turn). 
 - take resilient DEX to improve the chances that we succeed on the saving throw against sleet storm 
 - take a warlock dip at lvl-14 in order to eldritch mind and some pact magic slots that we can convert to sorcery points 
   in order to make our main combat tactic super consistent. Note that with CON save proficiency and eldritch mind, we'll 
   very rarely fail concentration checks even with CON=12 (deflect attacks also helps with this, since we can reduce the 
   DC of concentration checks when enemies hit us). 
 - Lvl-15-18 we go monk in order to get nice base class features, increase our martial arts die to a d10, and get an ASI. 
 - Use nature tattoo subclass feature to give yourself additional damage resistances. 
 - Finish out sorc for additional sorcery points and elemental affinity. End on warlock-02 in order to get alert and lucky from 
   lessons of the first ones.  
 - Overall, it's a simple mechanical concept wherein we use sentinel/grapple/nets to lock enemies inside a fog cloud or sleet 
   storm, and then make a bunch of attacks with advantage through the combination of the monk class (FoB) and nick mastery. 
   Late game investments in saving throws (eldritch mind CON, resilient/nature tattoo DEX, lucky) all facilitate a playstyle 
   where we can actually fight effectively within the sleet storm that we can cast. 
 - This is the best thing I could come up with that actually leverages the garbage that they give you in the tattooed monk 
   subclass. In the end I think it does a pretty good job and we end up with a cool/thematic build. But we'll essentially just 
   be a weird monk when we can't blind our enemies through heavily obscuring an area (i.e., if they have tremorsense or immunity 
   to the blinded condition). But that's not *that* common, so I think we should be fine.

Not bad for a melee sorcerer at lvl-01 actually. Squarely in C-level for tier-01 damage according to Treantmonk's rankings (07-09 DPR). For metamagic we pick extended spell and careful spell. We'll be using the former to boost uptime on our buff spells (like armor of agathys and magic weapon). And we'll use eventually use careful spell when we start casting sleet storm at later levels. The main draw here is the spell slot and sorcery points. With three lvl-01 spell slots and two sorcery points, we use one spell slot to cast mage armor, convert 2 sorcery points to 1 lvl-01 spell slot (3 avail) and then use all our remaining slots + our free cast to cast armor of agathys four times for up to 4hr of uptime. Note that it's probably more valuable to use our SP to create a new spell slot for an additional casting of armor of agathys, versus using them to extend the spell's duration, since it's more likely that we burn through the THP we get from casting it in combat than it is that the duration expires. This seems reasonable given that we are playing a melee striker build and have modest AC. I think it's also more reliable/flexible to cast the spell multiple times (when you think there might be danger) versus hoping that you can get multiple encounters out of a single cast + extended spell. For our new spell preparations we take comprehend languages and fog cloud. We'll want to start casting fog cloud at later levels, so we take it now. And comprehend languages is a ritual that is potentially very useful on a face character. Since we're using all our spell slots on armor of agathys, we don't really want to pick something that requires slots to cast in combat. Right, so then with 4 casts of armor of agathys per LR and no changes to monster AC or DEX saves, our new DPR calcs to: 
 (main-hand attack): (15/16)*(0.55*6.5 + 0.05*10)  = 3.82 dpr

Anyway, the point is that we are certainly tanky enough to wade into melee combat. And we're still dealing solid B-level damage (10-12 DPR) here in tier-01. Nice.

In terms of action economy, the idea is that on turn-01 of combat we (i) run up to an enemy and cast fog cloud, heavily obscuring a 20ft radius sphere around us, then (ii) use our BA to do flurry of blows, using one or both of our unarmed strikes to make a grapple attempt. Then (iii) on the enemy's turn, if we successfully grappled our target, they must use their action to try and break the grapple, remaining trapped in the fog cloud on a failure. Even if we failed our initial grapple attempt, or the enemy breaks free on their turn, if/when they try and move away from us (even if they disengage), sentinel allows us to use our reaction to make an opportunity attack (at advantage, because we're in the fog cloud) which, on hit, reduces the target's speed to zero ('freezes' them), keeping them trapped in the cloud. If we happen to roll super low when we make this opportunity attack and we have it available, we should use the heroic inspiration we get each LR from being a human to roll the AoO with triple advantage. This means that to avoid being trapped in the cloud, a target would have to succeed against our grapple at least once (2 chances) *and* avoid being hit by our sentinel attack, which we make with advantage or even triple advantage. For a quick back of the envelope, with a +3 to save against our grapple (and two chances to save) an enemy has about a ~70% chance of not being grappled. Then they also need to avoid being hit with our sentinel attack. Attacking with a +1 dagger against an AC-16 enemy, we have a 65% base chance to hit, an 88% chance to hit with advantage, and a 96% chance to hit with triple advantage. If we are just using the normal dagger, then we have a 60% base chance to hit, and 84% chance to hit with advantage, and a 94% chance to hit with triple advantage. Given this, let's just assume we have about an 85% chance to hit with our sentinel attack. Then, that means that there's a 15% chance of us missing.
