## Mechanical Concept Notes

### Overview

This build's combat identity revolves around **grappling targets**, dealing sustained melee damage through unarmed strikes and weapon attacks, and augmenting that damage and control with key warlock spells. The character plays as a rugged front-liner: hard to kill, hard to escape, and consistently threatening. The aesthetic blends the hand-to-hand discipline of a monk, the weapon mastery of the kensei and hexblade, and the macabre shadow-magic of a warlock.

The build has two phases defined by the concentration spell anchoring each:

- **Phase 1 (lvl-02 to lvl-09):** Concentrate on *hex*, leveraging monk's high attack volume to maximize hex damage while using it to impose disadvantage on enemy athletics/acrobatics checks to break your grapple.
- **Phase 2 (lvl-10 to lvl-20):** Transition to summon spells (*summon undead*, *summon aberration*) and eventually 5th-level spells, gaining versatility and passive damage/control while compensating for the loss of hex through hexblade subclass features.

---

### Why Grapple?

Grappling is the engine of this build. A grappled target has its speed reduced to 0, suffers disadvantage on attacks against anyone other than the grappler, and remains in place to be exploited by your spells and summons. The specific payoffs:

- **Control** — forced immobility, soft taunt, and positioning enemies near damaging summon auras or AoE effects
- **Damage** — grappler feat grants advantage on attacks against grappled targets, directly boosting DPR
- **High mobility + forced movement** — our base goliath speed (+5ft), large form (+10ft), monk unarmored movement, and step of the wind (BA dash) combine to give us exceptional mobility; once we acquire fast wrestler via the grappler feat, we can drag grappled targets at full speed, allowing us to reposition enemies very effectively
- **Hexblade synergy** — keeping enemies within 10ft maintains *accursed shield* (+2 AC), and grappled enemies are more likely to attack you, increasing the frequency of *unyielding will* procs (2d6 necrotic on concentration check success)
- **Summon synergy** — you can drag grappled targets into and keep them adjacent to your summoned creatures, maximizing passive aura effects (e.g., the mind flayer's *whispering aura* deals 2d6 psychic to nearby creatures each turn); grappled enemies have disadvantage on attacks against your summons, making them more durable. 

#### Making the Grapple Land

Grapple initiation and maintenance are reinforced throughout the build:

| Feature                  | Effect                                                                                   |
| ------------------------ | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Monk martial arts        | Grapple with DEX; BA unarmed strike to initiate                                          |
| Mind sliver + BA grapple | −1d4 penalty on the initial contested save                                               |
| Hex                      | Disadvantage on athletics/acrobatics checks to escape (Phase 1)                          |
| Grappler feat (lvl-06)   | Punch & grab: damage + grapple simultaneously; advantage on attacks vs. grappled targets |
| Hindering curse (lvl-11) | Hit with attack → disadvantage on target's next save, including grapple save             |
| Large form (lvl-05)      | Grapple Huge enemies; advantage on STR checks; +10ft move speed                          |
| Ray of enfeeblement      | Disadvantage on STR-based checks, including escape attempts                              |

The transition from Phase 1 to Phase 2 involves a deliberate trade: hex's grapple-escape disadvantage is dropped, but hindering curse makes grapple *initiation* more reliable. Net grapple effectiveness is comparable across both phases.

---

### Durability

Staying in melee while concentrating on a spell requires significant investment in survivability. This build stacks durability across three categories:

#### Hit Points & AC

- **Tough** (origin feat) — substantial HP increase throughout the build
- **Armor of shadows** (warlock-01) — cast *mage armor* at will; unarmored AC = 13 + DEX; provides good unarmored AC without requiring investment in WIS for monk unarmored defense
- **Agile parry** (kensei-03) — +2 AC when an unarmed strike is made as part of the Attack action
- **Sap** weapon mastery (longsword) — automatic disadvantage on the target's next attack roll on hit
- **Accursed shield** (hexblade-03) — +2 AC while within 10ft of the hexblade's curse target
- **Shield** (hexblade bound spell) — +5 AC as a reaction; emergency use only
- Combined: agile parry + accursed shield + sap mastery = effectively **+4 AC + imposed attack disadvantage** in most combats

#### Damage Reduction & Healing

- **Stone's endurance** (goliath) — reaction to reduce any incoming damage by 1d12 + CON, PB×/LR
- **Herbalism kit** (monk-01 proficiency) — craft healing potions between sessions
- **Uncanny metabolism** (monk-02) — heal for martial die + monk level once per day
- **Deflect attacks** (monk-03) — reduce incoming B/P/S damage from attack rolls by 1d10 + DEX + monk level, unlimited uses
- **Slow fall** (monk-04) — reduce fall damage by 5× monk level as a reaction
- **Hungering hex** (hexblade-03) — recover 1d8 + CHA HP when a cursed target dies, CHA mod times per day
- **Arcane vigor** (hexblade bound spell) — BA heal using hit dice; emergency use only
- **Heightened focus** (monk-10) — gain THP when using Patient Defense
- **Life drinker** (warlock invocation acquired at lvl-19) — expend hit dice to heal on hit with pact weapon

#### Concentration Protection

Protecting your concentration spell is essential to the build's function:

- **Eldritch mind** (warlock-02) — advantage on CON saves to maintain concentration
- **Way of the brush** (kensei-03) — proficiency with Calligrapher's supplies allows us to craft spell scrolls so we can maintain uptime on key spells even if our concentration is broken
- **Unyielding will** (hexblade-03) — auto-succeed on one failed concentration check per LR; gain 1d10 + warlock level THP on proc
- **Protection of the talisman** (warlock-07) — +1d4 to saving throws (including concentration) PB×/LR

By lvl-12, your average concentration check bonus — factoring in advantage (+3.5), protection of the talisman (+2.5), and your CON modifier (+2) — is approximately **+8**. Combined with our propensity for damage reduction, this should lead to a high probability of success on concentration checks.

### Offense

#### Phase 1: Hex + Martial Arts (lvl-02 to lvl-09)

The primary damage engine is hex's bonus damage across multiple attacks, combined with the monk's high attack volume from martial arts and Flurry of Blows. Key escalations:

- **lvl-05** — Kensei weapons; if you're comfortable forgoing agile parry, make one-handed attacks against a grappled target with a longsword to upgrade one attack to a d8 damage die; if not grappling, make attack with two hands to upgrade to a d10 damage die; longbow as a ranged weapon option 
- **lvl-06** — Grappler feat; punch & grab enables simultaneous damage + grapple; advantage on attacks vs. grappled targets
- **lvl-07** — Extra attack; martial arts (1d8); default to second attack action attack with longsword
- **lvl-08** — Hexblade's curse and unyielding will online; 2d6 necrotic on concentration check success

#### Phase 2: Summons + Hexblade Features (lvl-10 to lvl-20)

Hex is replaced by summon spells. Comparable damage to hex, but more versatile and durable — summoned creatures last longer (potentially across multiple combats), act as additional meat shields and deliver passive control/damage effects:

| Spell | Passive Effect | Synergy |
|---|---|---|
| Summon undead (putrid) | Festering aura: poisoned/potentially paralyzed | Grapple keeps enemies in aura |
| Summon undead (ghostly) | Frightened on hit | Grapple prevents frightened targets from moving away |
| Summon aberration (mind flayer) | Whispering aura: 2d6 psychic to creatures within 10ft | Drag grappled targets into the aura |

Additional Phase 2 damage features:

- **Tavern brawler** (lvl-10, via Lessons of the First Ones) — ~+0.5 damage per unarmed strike
- **Deft strike** (kensei-06, lvl-14) — spend 1 focus point for +1d8 damage; supplements FoB as a focus point expenditure option
- **Pact of the blade + life drinker** (lvl-19) — +1d6 necrotic on first pact weapon hit per turn
- **Sharpen the blade** (kensei-11, lvl-20) — spend focus points to grant any magic weapon without an inherent bonus up to +3 to attack and damage rolls

#### Phase 2, Second Half: 5th-Level Spells (lvl-19 to lvl-20)

At lvl-19, new concentration options become available. The best choice is situational:

| Spell                                    | Notes                                                                                                                                                                     |
| ---------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| *Animate objects* (hexblade bound spell) | Deals good BA damage if/when focus points are committed to sharpen the blade and deft strike, while still allowing you to field additional meat shields                   |
| *Jallarzi's storm of radiance*           | Best against spellcasters; stronger control (blinded/deafened/no spells) than summons                                                                                     |
| *Bestow curse* (upcast)                  | No concentration; can debuff both enemy saves against your initial grapple and continuing checks to escape it; can trigger hexblade's curse for action economy efficiency |
| *Summon undead/aberration* (upcast)      | Maximizes summon HP; doesn't compete with BA economy                                                                                                                      |

#### Additional Control Options

Beyond grappling, the build has access to a range of supplementary control tools:

- **Sap** weapon mastery
- **Wrathful smite** — frightened condition
- **Staggering smite** — prone condition
- **Ectoplasmic trail** — speed reduction
- **Ray of enfeeblement** — −1d8 to STR-based attack and damage rolls
- **Bestow curse** — wasted action or saving throw disadvantage, depending on option chosen
- **Jallarzi's storm of radiance** — blinded, deafened, and no spell casting

## Backstory & Setting

### Origins: Durath Keth

You were raised in Durath Keth — a small, ancient settlement at the northern edge of the Durath Fells, where the peaks break into moorland and the mist rarely lifts before midday. The Fells are severe and beautiful in the way of things that predate memory: black columns of volcanic rock rising from fog, glacial lakes that mirror skies changing by the hour, slopes of heather and pale birch thinning quickly into permanent snowfield. In summer the valley floors bloom with brief, violent color. In winter the passes close for months and the wind off the high ridges carries ice in it. The communities of the Fells — giantkin, dwarves, a scattering of wood elves who arrived generations ago and never left — are monastic and self-sufficient by nature, inclined toward stillness and distance from lowland politics. Durath Keth had no walls and no militia. People there kept livestock, brewed a dark heather ale, and minded their own affairs. Its eldest, a stone giantkin woman named Osa, taught calligraphy in the long winter months, led herb-gathering walks through the high passes each spring, and knew the name of every plant on the mountain — the common names and the old names, from the deep dialect that almost no one used anymore. Osa was the person who taught you the foundational movements of Azhari, the martial tradition practiced within the community for as long as anyone could remember.

Azhari was not a fighting art, exactly — not in Durath Keth. Its practitioners spoke of two fundamental forces running through all living things: Ael, the cohering force — order, endurance, the will that holds form against dissolution — and Narath, the dissolving force — entropy, change, the pressure that unmakes form and makes way for what comes next. Azhari taught that neither force was good or evil, that mastery lay in learning to touch both without being taken by either, and that the deepest power available to a living body belonged to someone who could tap into both forces simultaneously and hold them in parallel. In practice, in Durath Keth, the Ael arts were taught to children and techniques that tapped into Narath were approached slowly, over years, with great care. The tradition was a meditative martial practice first, a combat discipline second. It had produced no soldiers for generations. It produced you.

---

### The Coming of the Varenthi Legion

The Sulvaric Compact arrived in the Fells not because of anything to do with Durath Keth, but because of a prolonged insurgency amongst the Durath clans in the hill-territories to the east that had destabilized the Compact's northern trade routes for the better part of a decade. The Varenthi Legion — one of the Compact's great regional forces, drawn from the interior highland provinces and distinguished by their engineering efficiency and logistical precision — was dispatched to end it.

The Compact itself was old and genuinely formidable: a pan-continental institution with a deliberative Senate, a codified legal tradition, and Legions of markedly different cultural character — some raised from the arid and windswept southern provinces, some from the densely-populated eastern archipelagos, some from the deep interior like the Varenthi. The Compact was not a gentle institution, but it was, on balance, a competent and mostly just one. With an iron fist and tactical precision, the Varenthi Legion pacified the hill-territories within three years and established a durable stability across the Fells. The mountain passes became safer. Trade opened up. Durath Keth flourished.

The Varenthi established a permanent garrison at Halvenmoor, the market town at the base of the Fells. You went down to see it, young — not yet twenty years of age — and ended up enlisting in the Durath Foederati, a mixed frontier regiment drawing from local communities to supplement the Legion's professional core. You were young enough that some of the other recruits called it a waste. You were good enough quickly enough to shut them up. The physical discipline suited you. The camaraderie suited you. The sense of purpose suited you. You were not a prodigy, but you were steady and genuinely well-liked — possessing an easy warmth and directness that moved well up and down the chain of command.

---

### Aldara Maren

Legate Aldara Maren was the Varenthi Legion's senior field commander and the architect of the hill-territory pacification. Maren represented the very best of the Compact: brilliant tactically, fair in the field, and possessed of a dry, quick-witted intelligence that made people feel simultaneously assessed and at ease. She had a way of delivering devastating observations with the mildest expression, and of finding the exact thing to say in a briefing room to make arrogant, politically-appointed senior officers swallow their words and question their priors. Soldiers adored her. Her reputation preceded her into every garrison she entered.

You served in her broader command without distinction — one among thousands — but even so you were able to feel the touch of her leadership, to see how it influenced the Legion at every level, and to understand what her reputation was built on. It was real.

Then she came into possession of the weapon.

The blade had been recovered during the hill-territory campaign, pulled from what seemed like a tomb beneath a fortress of indeterminate age that no one could identify in the regional records. The Compact's arcanists catalogued it and flagged it for review; Maren argued, persuasively, that she was the appropriate person to hold it — that an artifact of unclear martial provenance should be in the hands of someone capable of wielding it responsibly and reporting on its properties. The review board agreed. This was, in retrospect, the precise argument the weapon would have made on its own behalf.

Soldiers who saw her use it in the field gave it a name, whispered to each other in a register somewhere between awe and unease: the Ashen Bride. It was beautiful in the manner of a thing that knows it is being watched — its long, slender odachi blade holding a faint luminescence in low light, its edge keen beyond any natural maintenance, its balance so perfect it felt almost weightless despite its length, and seemed to move of its own accord in a practiced hand. But there was something unnerving in the quality of that beauty. The light it gave off was pale rather than warm. The keenness of the edge suggested appetite rather than craft. The intention of the blade conferred an undertow — as if it were not enhancing its wielder's speed and precision, but quietly replacing them with its own. In Maren's hands, the Ashen Bride earned her name: transcendent and brutal, her silvered skirts stained black with blood.

Arcanists who later studied it properly would describe its essential quality as the Thrill of Erasure: a progressive substitution of the wielder's own interiority — doubts, history, attachments, the ordinary friction of a person with a conscience — with the weapon's single, consuming desire: the absolute dominance of one will over all others. In Azhari's terms, it was Narath in its most untempered form — not dissolution in balance with Ael, but dissolution consuming everything, entropy without the counterweight of cohesion to hold against it. The weapon's true name was Kaziru — a word from a language predating the Compact by millennia, translating roughly as: *the silence after the last thing breaks*.

Maren fell slowly. The tactical brilliance remained, but curdled with contempt. Iron-fisted justice hardened into cruel punishment. Sharp and disarming wit transformed into Machiavellian manipulation and brash bullying. You were still in her command when the atrocities began — a Durath clan settlement in the high passes burned for alleged intelligence ties to hill-territory holdouts. Compact prisoners executed without tribunal. Ancestral grave sites desecrated. You told yourself you were just following orders, but you knew that your hands were not clean.

---

### The Narathi Rending

Maren's formal break from the Compact — her declaration of independence posted to the gate of the Halvenmoor garrison — started what came to be known as the Narathi Rending. It began with the indoctrination of officers into Maren's personal philosophy of martial hierarchy, progressed to the purging of loyalists within the Legion's command structure, and escalated into a full secessionist insurrection. The philosophy that emerged among Maren's forces, articulated in her addresses to the troops and subsequently evangelized by her inner circle — a group of elite soldiers known as the Skavareth — was essentially the theology of untempered Narath: that order and cohesion were illusions imposed by institutions to preserve their own power; that the eradicating force was the only honest force; that true martial mastery meant embracing destruction as liberation. In practice, what remained of the Legion during the Rending operated like a cult, with Maren at its head.

During the Rending, Maren trained her forces in disciplines drawn from Kaziru — techniques that channeled Narath not with Azhari's care but which opened oneself fully to its undiluted force. Necrotic strikes. Shadow-movement. The binding of fallen spirits to a living will. Informally it was known as the Legate's art. As a junior soldier you received fragments of this training, enough to feel the current and to understand what it was, not enough to be fully drawn in. The Skavareth, by the end, became something else entirely. Physically and spiritually transformed by its practice. You were never among Maren's chosen, but what you witnessed during the rebellion scarred you indelibly. You held lines while terrible things happened behind them. You followed orders you knew were wrong because Aldara Maren had been the finest officer you had ever served under, and some part of you kept waiting for that person to return. She didn't.

---

### Betrayal: Becoming an Informant of the Secundan Office

The Sulvaric Compact's intelligence apparatus is a lateral body called the Secundan Office. It operates independently of any single Legion and answers to an oversight council within the Senate. The Office had been tracking the Rending from its earliest stages. They worked in institutional blind spots: cultivating assets inside organizations, moving carefully through channels conventional military intelligence couldn't reach. You don't know how they found you, or how they knew of your misgivings. But less than a year into the Rending, you "happened" to run into an Office operative named Sera Dundas at one of the local pubs in Halvenmoor. Understated, precise, and better at reading people than anyone you had met, Dundas knew exactly what to say to convince you to work with her. Over the following months you fed the Office what you had access to through a dead-drop in the Halvenmoor market — troop movements, descriptions of the Legate's arts used by the Skavareth, the location of the Rending's secondary supply node. The critical engagement — a loyalist ambush at the Erindal Ford that severed the Rending's supply lines and isolated Maren's primary force — was built in part on your information. At the final battle on the Vethric Plain, you turned your blade openly. You killed people who had been in your unit. Some had become true believers in what Kaziru had made of Maren's vision. Some were just people who had nowhere else to go. That day haunts you still.

---

### The Execution: What Maren Left Behind

Maren was captured at the Vethric Plain. The Ashen Bride's fate at the moment of her defeat is genuinely unclear. Some said Secundan operatives had recovered it and moved it to a secure facility; some said it dissolved into smoke when she fell; one soldier, who never recanted and was never fully believed, swore he had watched the blade sink into Maren's body itself, absorbing into her chest as if the flesh had drawn it in. The Compact made no official statements regarding the weapon. No one has seen it since.

Maren was transported to the Compact's regional administrative center at Solvenmere, tried before a military tribunal, and sentenced to death. You served on the security detail holding her in the Solvenmere garrison through the weeks between sentencing and execution — a strange, close assignment no one else wanted.

Stripped of Kaziru, Maren was no longer lucid. What remained was someone whose extraordinary mind was visibly failing without the presence that had sustained — or consumed — it for years. She ranted, went quiet for hours and then spoke in fragments that seemed to address people who weren't present. She laughed at things no one else could hear. Occasionally, for a few minutes at a time, something coherent would surface — the ghost of the intelligence she had been — and in those windows she said things you have been turning over ever since. About Kaziru's ancient power, drawn from a source the Compact's arcanists had not found and would not find by looking where they were looking. About a counterpart — another weapon, a mirror drawn from the same source as Kaziru, which she described as the **emptiness that endures** and **the unbreakable nothingness**. About some sort of ticking clock — a plan or a consequence or a transformation, you could never fully tell which — that would inevitably play out following her death.

You do not know how much of these musings were real and how much was the fracturing of a tortured mind. You have not been able to stop thinking about what she said.

One detail stays with you that had nothing to do with her words. In the weeks of her captivity, Maren had grown one fingernail to an unnatural length — the left pinky, blackened and slightly curved, faintly iridescent at the tip, bearing an uncanny resemblance in its cold luminescent quality to the Ashen Bride itself. During one of her more volatile episodes, she seized your wrist through the bars and drew the nail across the back of your hand before you could pull away. The scratch felt icy cold for a week. And while it left no visible mark, the chill it left in your left hand has never gone away.

Maren was executed three days later and her body burned on a pyre in accordance with the traditions of her homeland. You were present in the yard. In the early hours the following day, you collected a small amount of her ashes in an ornate metal box. 

---

### Aftermath: Durath Keth to Aldenmere

The Secundan Office gave you a formal commendation and an informal separation. Sera Dundas shook your hand and did not quite meet your eyes. You were not yet twenty-five years old.

You went back to Durath Keth once. The Rending's violence had reached the high passes — raids, burned homesteads, settlements torn apart for suspected loyalties. Osa was among the dead. The community recognized you. It did not welcome you. You left before anyone had to ask.

You found work with the Thornback Company, a mid-sized mercenary outfit operating out of the lowland city of Aldenmere. They do not ask questions. The pay is reliable. Most days, that is enough.

You are in Aldenmere when the campaign begins. You have been here long enough to know the streets and the taverns and the particular quality of quiet that follows a hard contract. You have not been back to the Fells in years. The chill in your left hand has not faded. Nor have your memories of what you witnessed during the Rending. Some nights you feel a pull in a direction you cannot name, and you lie awake in the dark of whatever room you are renting and try to decide if that means anything. It probably means something.

---

## Character Themes, Progression & DM Plot Hooks

### Character Arc: Averath

To keep your demons at bay — you commit yourself to building something. It begins from Azhari: the discipline of Ael, the careful and respectful approach to Narath, the foundational teaching that both forces can be inhabited without being consumed. But it goes somewhere Durath Keth's practitioners were never willing to — into the deliberate interweaving of both currents simultaneously, held not in careful separation but in dynamic proximity. Not two streams touching at a crossing-point. Something more like a whirlpool — Ael and Narath in close orbit, their opposition and attraction held in tension, the energy of that mutual pulling turned into something graceful, precise, and dangerous. The two forces not canceling each other but winding tighter, each making the other more potent, the whole greater than either current alone.

You call it Averath — from the same ancient root as Narath, the *ave* prefix carrying something like *turning* or *coiling*: the place where the waters spiral. It is a weapon-integrated discipline, building on the basic techniques you learned as a soldier, the dark arts you began to learn under Maren's tutelage, and the hand-and-body foundation of Azhari. Practically, it is how you survive and excel in combat. Therapeutically, it is how you process what happened — the shame, the moral injury, the question of whether someone who did what you did deserves to find their way back to themselves.

At the start of the campaign, Averath is nascent — more idea than system, more instinct than form. Your abilities reflect a foundation: the physical conditioning of a young Azhari disciple, the basic martial training of a Compact soldier, and the awareness of an unsettling, otherworldly power from training in Maren's dark arts. Maren's scratch represents a node of potential. The Narath current inside you is present but unexamined, perhaps surfacing in flashes you don't fully control. The kensei work — the weapon mastery, the refinement of technique — is something you are actively building. The hexblade connection is something you are only beginning to understand.

As the campaign progresses, Averath deepens. The weapon discipline sharpens. The Narath current becomes something you can reach for deliberately rather than something that reaches for you. The whirlpool becomes more stable — or more precisely, you become better at standing inside it. What begins as an augmentation of your strikes and a sharpening of your instincts will, over time, reveal itself as a genuine connection to the same ur-source from which Kaziru and its counterpart drawn. The kensei controls the hexblade. The hexblade empowers the kensei. Whether this character can maintain the balance is something to be revealed as the campaign unfolds, and a question every practitioner of Averath must answer for themselves each and every day.

Somewhere ahead, maybe, is a school — a place and a tradition for people like you, for soldiers with blood on their hands and a genuine desire to be something other than what that blood suggests. A place where Averath can be taught to those who need it. You hold that aspiration loosely.

---

### Core Themes

- **Kaziru and Its Unresolved Fate** — The weapon is the spine of everything. Its fate after the Vethric Plain is genuinely unknown — contained, dissolved, or absorbed into Maren's body depending on who you ask. It may be in a vault. It may be waiting.
- **Moral Injury and Complicity** — You held the line while a Durath clan settlement burned. You killed people at the Vethric Plain who were simply lost. This is not guilt as a personality trait — it is an open wound that the right encounter reopens.
- **Can the Whirlpool Hold?** — The splinter of Narath inside you grows with use and with danger. Averath's premise — that both forces can be held in dynamic tension, that the whirlpool can be controlled — is the answer you are building. The campaign should test it, repeatedly, without ever settling the question cleanly.
- **Exile and the Construction of Home** — Durath Keth is closed to you. The Compact discarded you. The Thornback Company is a placeholder. The through-line of your arc is the slow construction of something you actually believe in — Averath, the school, the tradition — built for people the world has damaged and discarded.
- **The Ronin's Code** — You are a warrior without a master, still deciding what fills that absence. Your compass is real. The campaign should test what it is made of.

---

### DM Plot Hooks

- **Kaziru Moves** — Word reaches you — through the Secundan Office, through a dream, through someone who recognizes the quality of the coldness in your left hand — that the weapon has surfaced again. Or that whatever official account existed of its containment has quietly come apart. Or that a commander somewhere has begun to show signs.

- **Zurenu, "the emptiness that endures"** — Maren's ravings placed the mirror weapon somewhere in the old territories. The splinter in your left hand has begun pulling — not painfully, but directionally — toward something you cannot yet name. A job takes you close enough that ignoring it becomes a choice rather than a default.

- **Sera Dundas Has a Job** — The Secundan Office operative who recruited you reappears in Aldenmere with an unofficial ask — something the Compact cannot be seen doing. She needs your combination of capability and deniability. The complication is serious.

- **The Skavareth** — Maren's inner circle was never fully accounted for after the Rending. Some died at the Vethric Plain. Some didn't. Someone bearing the marks of their training — untempered Narath, the Legate's art in its most advanced form — has surfaced somewhere, and the trail leads toward you.

- **A Survivor of the Rending** — You encounter someone who served under Maren, ended up on the wrong side of the Vethric Plain, and lived. They may want redemption, answers, or to finish something unfinished. How you handle it says something about who you are now.

- **The Student / The Inheritor** — Someone has been watching you train and asks to learn about Averath. This is the first stone of the school, if you choose to lay it. It is also a weight you have not carried before. Alternatively, someone — a student, or someone who observed your techniques and reconstructed them badly — has taken what they understood of Averath and built something else from it: the whirlpool without the discipline, or perhaps a rediscovery of the Legate's art. They are gathering followers. At some point they become a problem you cannot avoid, because whatever they are doing is partly because of you.

- **Figment of the Fingernail** — In a moment of extreme duress — a near-death experience, a confrontation with something necromantic — the scratch freezes on your hand and you experience something that is not your own memory: a fragment of Maren's, or Kaziru's, or something older than either. It may be a revelation. It may be a new question. Probably both.

- **Durath Keth** — Something draws you back — trouble in the high passes, a letter from a friend or community member, a contract through the Fells. Osa's grave is there. The community's grief is real and your role in what happened to them is complicated. Reconciliation, if it comes, will not come in a single visit.

- **Contact from the Compact** — Someone above Dundas reaches out with something that looks like an olive branch. Maybe it's an opportunity, or maybe it's an attempt to close a loose end. One way or the other, you know the Secundan Office didn't earn its mysterious and intimidating reputation by leaving former assets fully autonomous.

>[!note]
Lvl-01 and we start out monk-01. Not only is this thematically appropriate, given our characters upbringing in Durath Keth, but I think starting monk conveys the most mechanical advantages as well. 
>
Probably the most impactful of these is the fact that starting out in monk gives us an extra tool proficiency, which we can use to get proficiency with the herbalism kit. This will allow us to craft healing potions (as well as antitoxins and healer's kits) during our downtime. Having a few extra healing potions on hand will be very helpful for our character, who will be tanking hits as a front-line fighter. 
>
Starting monk also lets us get the best and most thematic set of starting skill proficiencies. Specifically, we get acrobatics and stealth from monk, and athletics and intimidation from our soldier background. This seems like the right set for a simple, likeable, but perhaps also a bit menacing character. Also note that with our powerful build species feature, we will have both proficiency and advantage on acrobatics checks to end a grapple. So even here at lvl-01, we are more specialized into grappling than most other characters. 
>
In terms of equipment, since we won't be using weapons for a while, and will never have use of armor, we spend our starting gold on utility items. Specifically, we pick up an herbalism kit as well as a set of calligrapher's supplies. We aren't proficient with the calligrapher's supplies yet, but we will be eventually so we might as well get them here. Same goes for the arcane focus, which we'll start using next level. Nets are quite useful, especially at early levels and will work well with our high DEX. And we can use the perfume if we need to turn up the charm and leverage our pretty decent CHA. I also opted to start out with a Pole of Collapsing, which is a common magic item that functions as a pole (10ft long, provides advantage on athletics/acrobatics checks when jumping) that can collapse down to something 1ft long (and thus portable). This seems like a natural/logical item for a young athletic and acrobatic character from the crags of Durath Fells to have on hand. Perhaps it has some special origin related to our backstory/upbringing. Mechanically, it just gives us a bit more mobility, especially in terrain similar to our homeland, which I think is evocative. Replace this with a potion of healing (same gold cost) if your DM doesn't allow the purchase of magic items at character creation. 
>
For feats and features we start out with the tough origin feat and the stone's endurance ability from Stone Goliath. These work together to make our character more durable than most others at lvl-01, even despite our low CON modifier (+1). Stone's endurance is particularly impactful at low levels. Even with just +1 in CON, this feature provides 2d12+2 (15) HP of damage reduction per long-rest which more than doubles our effective hit point total. This will be needed since our AC is pretty weak here at lvl-01. We get unarmored defense as a monk base class feature, but with our low WIS we only end up with AC-14. It's not terrible, and it's lvl-01 so we should be fine. But another reason we appreciate having tough and stone's endurance. 
>
Offensively, we rely entirely on the martial arts monk feature here at lvl-01. This feature lets us use DEX for the attack and damage rolls of our unarmed strikes, allows us to make an unarmed strike as a bonus action, lets us replace the damage die of our unarmed strikes with our martial arts die, which at this level is 1d6. Critically, martial arts also lets us use DEX for the save DC of the grapple and shove options of unarmed strike. 
>
In terms of playstyle, things are super straightforward here to start out. We're just a basic lvl-01 monk. Our AC is a little lower than your standard monk, but we have some additional survivability features to make up for it. In combat, we just use our action to attack and make unarmed strikes, and use our BA to do another unarmed strike. Grapple enemies and use nets if you want to impose some basic control. Use your perfume for out-of-combat social scenarios.
