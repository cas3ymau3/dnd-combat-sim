MECHANICAL CONCEPT NOTES
 - get psychic weapon and BA attack via soul knife 
 - sharpened mind psionic discipline gives you scaling damage die replacement for psychic damage 
 - savage attacker gives you damage reroll on weapon attacks 
 - savage attacker + sharpened mind adds ~1d8 to your basic attack combo while you take psion levels (equivalent to 2 levels of rogue)
 - metamorph gives you extra attack and viscera launcher weapon, which also adds 2d6+DEX damage (equivalent to 6 levels of rogue) 
 - metamorph also gives you THP generation, healing spells, and increased durability via mutable form 
 - finish out fighter to get medium armor, dueling and thrown weapon fighting flat damage bonuses (+4 to all dagger attacks) and increased 
   crit chance; higher crit chance disproportionately benefits our damage because of our damage dice upgrading via savage attacker/sharpened mind 
 - overlap in functionality between psion and soul knife means you can target psionic dice resources more efficiently -- ex. since 
   you get powerful telepathy from soul knife, you can focus psion dice on inerrant aim; similarly, since we can use rogue dice 
   to add psionic dice to ability checks, we don't have to take psionic disciplines to boost our skill-monkey capacity 
 - mage slayer + psionic guards + intellect fortress (spell) safeguards your mental saves  
 - go (relatively) low INT and cast primarily healing/utility/mobility spells
 - build CON for melee durability; high CON also strengthens breath weapon, which contributes a small amount to overall damage scaling 
 - overall, you're a bit less than a half caster, kind of like a better version of an arcane trickster (1/3 caster), where you sacrifice 
   pure rogue scaling for the extra damage you get from the subclass synergies 
 - build hinges upon one key interpretation of metamorph wording. Specifically, the organic weapons feature says that: 
   	"When you take the Attack action, you can use this feature before you make the attack roll. Your limb retains the 
         form of the organic weapon until ...you return the limb to its previous form (no action required)."
   and the psychic blades feature of the soul knife says: 
   	"Whenever you take the Attack action or make an Opportunity Attack, you can manifest a Psychic Blade in your free 
         hand and make the attack with that blade...After you attack with the blade on your turn, you can make a melee or
         ranged attack with a second psychic blade as a Bonus Action on the same turn if your other hand is free to create it."  
 - so the key interpretation here is as follows: assume that we have nothing in our hands at the start of our turn; then we 
   (i) take the attack action and, per organic weapons, transform our `first' hand into a viscera launcher and (ii), per psychic
   blades, manifest a psychic blade in our `second' (free) hand, then (iii) attack with the viscera launcher and, per organic 
   weapons, revert the limb back to its original form at no action cost so it's free for the BA psychic blade and then (iv) 
   attack with the initial psychic blade (the one in our `second hand'), triggering the BA attack which we make with our `first' 
   hand now that it's reverted back to it's original form. Overall this allows us to make two psychic blade attacks and our 
   viscera launcher attack on our turn.

NOTE: You might think we should go rogue-04 to get our first feat/ASI. But no, instead we're going psion-02 to pick up two psionic disciplines. Both are going to be important for us in the long run, but sharpened mind is the big one. Because we are doing multiple instances of psychic damage on our turn (between our weapon, sneak attack, and BA attack it's 4 separate die rolls) there is a high chance that one of those rolls will be less than our sharpened mind roll. This greatly increases the value of sharpened mind and this stacks on top of the damage high-grading we are already gaining from savage attacker. On average, sharpened mind + savage attacker will increase our DPR by ~2-3, which is a bigger jump than we would get from just bumping our DEX modifier by 1. And while boosting DEX would also increase our chance to hit, we did also just get a proficiency bonus increase. And we picked up inerrant aim which we can use to make sure our initial psychic blade attack lands and applies vex. Then, with advantage, we still have a very good chance to hit. Now we don't yet have the quantity of psionic dice to do this often. If we are in 4 combats per day and want to use sharpened mind in each of them, that leaves us with only 1 use of inerrant aim (assuming 1 SR per day). But we will eventually get more psionic dice, and at least some days we probably will be in fewer than 4 full combats. `But what about the defense boost from bumping DEX?', you ask. Well, going psion-02 also gets us another spell slot, meaning we can cast shield an additional time per day. That isn't quite the same value as a permanent +1 to AC, but it's not nothing. And as a ranged fighter, hopefully we aren't getting attacked all that often. So all in all, going psion-02 here boosts our damage, chance to hit, and defensive capacity in a similar manner to a boost to our DEX mod. And it's more fun to get our core multiclass features online sooner rather than later, versus just taking a boring boost to DEX. So yeah, that's why we do that here.

NOTE: Alright, finishing off our psion levels, we go psion-08 and pick up resilient (CON) and boost our CON to 17. Now that we've maxed our DEX, we have to choose what secondary stat to invest in. Obviously, as a psion, we could focus on INT and then continue with psion levels in order to become more of a full caster. I would say the build is technically `complete' at this level. The last 3rd of our development is more about boosting our damage scaling while maintaining our character's combat/overall identity. NOTE: Alright, pivot level here. We're level 13 and we go fighter-01. You might be wondering why we go fighter instead of dipping back to rogue in order to pick up another 1d6 sneak attack dice, cunning strike, uncanny dodge, and move closer to all the juicy higher-level rogue base class features (expertise, reliable talent, evasion, etc.). Well the main reason is that base rogue damage scaling isn't good. And the soul knife subclass isn't going to give us anything else that will help our damage scaling other than sneak attack. I mean, it's just +1d6 every 2 levels. So while the defensive/out-of-combat features are good (and they are), if we want to do real damage into tier-03 and tier-04, then we'll have to seek greener pastures. And, in this case it's with fighter levels. For example, right off the bat at fighter-01 we get to pick a fighting style, and we go with dueling. Given our attack mechanics, we will only ever make attacks with our psychic blades when we are holding no other weapons, so the dueling damage bonus will always apply. As a bonus, if/when we use our bone-blade in melee the dueling bonus will also apply to those attacks (this is why we favor dueling over two-weapon fighting here). Given that we make two psychic blade attacks per turn, this is adding a flat +4 to our DPR, and that is already *more* than what we would get from going rogue-05. In addition, fighter-01 also gets us second wind for a bit of healing each day (helps offset not getting uncanny dodge), and some extra proficiencies (medium armor, shields, all martial weapons) and additional weapon masteries. The proficiencies and masteries are not important. Though I suppose it does give us the ability to use a broader suite of magic armor/weapons/items if these happen to be in our game. For our simulation purposes, we're going to assume that we continue to use mage armor and only our psychic blades/organic weapons. This is mostly for flavor/thematic consistency. We haven't needed armor/weapons for so long, it feels weird to pick them up now and we don't really need to. But I suppose it's nice to have options. So yeah, pretty straightforward level in terms of simulated DPR. We get the flat boost from the dueling fighting style and we also get a proficiency bonus increase, meaning greater chance to hit and more breath attacks per day, which also increases our damage a bit. Nice. Going fighter is also nice since the d10 hit die will help give us more HP as we progress. Thematically, I think we like fighter more than continuing in rogue since our character's identity is that of overwhelming natural power, cunning, and ability. At this point, we aren't really some delicate rogue. We are a psionic warrior, fueled by our own physical excellence and martial prowess. Lean in to this kind of identity as we progress further.
