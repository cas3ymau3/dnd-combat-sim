Has speed of 50ft, size is medium (so we, as a small creature, can mount it) has 60ft of darkvision, and has the agile feature - meaning it doesn't provoke opportunity attacks when it moves out of enemies reach. It also has DEX=16 (+3), meaning if we cast mage armor on it, it will have an AC of 16, which is pretty good (better than ours). In addition, with its +2 WIS modifier and proficiency in perception, our deer mount does have a +4 to perception, which is going to be better than our own perception for the entirety of our character's career. So traveling with our mount (who can telepathically communicate with us) will provide us with a small passive boost to this fairly important skill. Now, the deer only has 4 HP, so it is currently very fragile. And if we provoke opportunity attacks while riding it, creatures can still target the deer. If our DM allows it, I would suggest using our leatherworker's tools to craft this for ourselves. Doing so would only take 2 days (16hr) of downtime and 10gp of raw materials. Alright then for spellcasting, we get two bard cantrips and four bard spells. For the cantrips, go with blade ward and vicious mockery. Using these both at the same time allows us to be pretty defensive if we need to be. Then for spells, we pick up detect magic for extra utility without requiring spell slots, find familiar from the mark of handling spell list to create our mount, healing word and heroism. We take healing word since we want to keep our action open for dealing damage w/true strike but do want a way to heal our mount if it takes damage. Then, we take heroism so we can cast it on our mount giving it +3 THP each turn and immunity to frightened. We also get bardic inspiration, which we can give to our familiar to help it with saving throws (or ability checks). Between inspiration, mage armor, heroism, and healing word, our mount *should* be quite durable for lvl-02 and having 50ft of movement speed should let us kite our enemies very effectively which will increase our own survivability. Lean into the flavor of being a mounted cowboy character here. Oh and one last note on the mount mechanics -- the find familiar spell states that our familiar should have its own initiative in combat, which would make things a lot messier. However, the 2024 PHB rules on controlled mounts state that your mount shares your initiative when you mount it. So we are assuming that because we are treating our familiar as a controlled mount, it will share our initiative in combat and act on our turn (immediately after us). I don't think this is particularly controversial, or even a subjective interpretation of the rules at all. But do check with your DM to make sure this is how it works, because otherwise running the mount familiar will just be a headache.

At character lvl-05 we also get cantrip scaling, meaning our hand crossbow true-strike attacks now deal 2d6+CHA (10) on hit. For spellcasting, we get one new bard preparation, access to level-2 bard spells, and two level-3 spell slots. For the preparation, we take aid as a second level spell and use it once each day to increase our mount's (+2 other allies') health by 10. Now our deer mount has 14 HP, making it tanky enough to take a hit or fail a saving throw and not die, especially if we're boosting its health with heroism. In combination mage armor + our combat inspiration, we should be doing enough to keep our mount alive throughout the day. Then, we also want to switch out healing word for cure wounds in our bard preparations. Now that our mount has a bit more health it will be more effective to heal it with cure wounds -- which is a stronger heal and upcasts well -- than it will be to use healing word. Plus, we can also now use our familiar to deliver cure wounds (a touch spell) to allies if need be. In particular, since the deer doesn't provoke opportunity attacks (agile feature), we can dismount and it can run straight across the battle field and deliver cure wounds very effectively. Another nice synergy of this build that makes the mount feel special/powerful. Ok yeah, so overall a pretty defensive level put definitely we feel a lot stronger. And the defensive buffs allow us to feel pretty good about kiting in around the battlefield and getting close enough to enemies so that we can shoot them with our crossbow. Use your high level slots on aid, witch bolt, cure wounds, and heroism (which you can upcast and then augment with extended spell). Witch bolt is your default offensive option, heroism your default defensive one. Keep your first level slots for mage armor, jump, and shield. Provide great utility with skill proficiency and rituals. Damage wise, with witch bolt active we can do 2d6+1d12+CHA = 16.5 dmg each round if we're feeling punchy. Quite low (D-class), but we aren't really building for damage and it's early days here for tier-02. Plus, if we consider that our allies are using some of our combat inspiration to deal damage, then we're also contributing a bit more that way.

When enemies enter the emanation or end their turn within it they are forced to make a CON save or take 3d6 (10..5) thunder damage and become deafened (half damage on save). Second, we really like the defensive buff given that we are going to be trying to leverage our high mobility to keep our enemies at range in combat (making it more likely that they'll have to make ranged attack rolls against us). Third, it synergizes quite well with our high-mobility playstyle in AOE. If there are multiple enemies on the field, we can use our mount's high speed (80ft if we have longstrider and jump active) to tag multiple creatures with the spell's damage and still retreat to safety. This makes the modest damage of the spell on paper more effective in practice for our character. The main competition here is with conjure animals -- which does more/more reliable damage (3d10 slashing on a DEX save and upcasts better), but is limited to 30ft of movement per turn, and provides a worse defensive buff (adv on STR saves while within 10ft of the animal pack). In the end, both spells would function similarly for us, but I like the fact that cacophonic shield better leverages aspects of our character build in which we've already invested significant resources -- namely the high mobility afforded by our signature mount. As we've said multiple times in this guide already, this character is not focused on damage so I think we appreciate these synergies and the better defensive buff provided by cacophonic shield more than the extra damage we would get from conjure animals. Can't say I've ever loved the style/theme of conjure animals either, though I think it would be a good fit on this character. Note, it might even be a good move to try and do a flyby with cacophonic shield on an enemy and try to provoke an opportunity attack just so you can cast shield and give yourself that defensive buff for the duration of the round allowing you to do drive-by's on other enemies and waste their reaction while also dealing damage (though you couldn't do this on the turn you cast it because you can only cast one spell w/a spell slot on your turn). This won't always be relevant, but when you really think about it, there is usually some form of flora or fauna in almost every DnD setting -- whether that's wild animals, domesticated animals, natural or horticultured vegetation, or something from the relatively lengthy list of monstrosities with which we can communicate (see lvl-03 note above). Specifically, we can use find familiar to conjure a vulture (reskin/flavor however you want -- maybe a giant crow?) which is a medium-sized beast with a walking speed of 10ft and a fly speed of 50ft. However, unlike the deer, the vulture only has DEX=10, so mage armor would only bring its AC up to 13, which isn't quite good enough. Barkskin, however, would give our vulture mount an AC of 17 for 1hr, and up to 2hr with extended spell. With the AC boost from our defensive combat inspiration which it will now (hopefully) always have access to, this means our mount will have an effective AC of 22.5. All that said, with how we've built this character, if we use the vulture mount we will effectively be giving ourselves a permanent flying speed of 50ft for the entire adventuring day. And RAW we can still cast jump to boost our vulture's speed by an additional 20ft (up to 70ft) when needed even if we're flying. That's pretty awesome. By comparison, at lvl-14 a draconic sorcerer gets their `dragon wings' subclass feature, which gives them 60ft fly speed as a BA for 1hr per day (recover on LR or w/3 SP). And that's the entire subclass feature! We're getting a better version of that with magical secrets here, which is a feature which we will continue to benefit from at future levels. Furthermore, a lot of the downsides of the vulture (relative to the deer) are somewhat mitigated by the fact that it's a flying creature. For example, flying makes it much less likely that we will provoke opportunity attacks. And finally, it's also worth noting that AOE spells that deal damage in cones -- like dragon's breath -- are more effective on flying creatures. This is because of how cones work in 5e. Specifically, the rules on cone AOEs in the 2024 PHB state that a 15ft cone spell produces a cone that is 15ft wide 15ft from its point of origin. This means that if we attack from directly overhead of an enemy, the footprint of the spell on the ground will be a circle with a 15ft diameter, which is a significantly greater surface area than the cone if we cast it on the ground. Bigger AOE means we'll be able to catch more enemies and deal more damage. All-in-all, it's just a very powerful combination. Now whenever we cast shield, jump, command, cure wounds, animal friendship, barkskin, prayer of healing, spider climb, magic weapon (if @ lvl-02), speak with plants, locate object, freedom of movement, locate creature, dominate beast (if @ lvl-04), grasping vine, or even if we're upcasting dragon's breath/spiritual weapon there's a chance we'll get our spell slot back. Since we are definitely going to be using some of the spells every day regardless -- ex. we're going to cast barkskin at least once or twice per day, and we're definitely casting prayer of healing once per day -- we'll get good value out of this feat. All in all, I would expect to get between 1-4 spell slots back each day. In addition, with font of magic we can convert unused spell slots into sorcery points if we want/need to, meaning we can used twinned spell and extended spell more frequently. So you could also think of this feat as giving us extra sorcery points each day. All in all, it's just making us better at stuff we're already doing, and relaxing the constraints we might face between casting utility spells and doing damage/providing maximum support.
